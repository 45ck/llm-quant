# Hypothesis H6.5: Multi-Asset Carry Rotation

hypothesis_id: "H6.5"
family: 6 — Macro Regime Rotation
created_at: "2026-03-31"
status: "hypothesis_written"

## Statement

Ranking 5 cross-asset ETFs (SPY, TLT, GLD, EFA, EEM) by their trailing
carry proxy — defined as the 63-day risk-adjusted return (return / realized
vol) — and going long the top 2 while avoiding the bottom 3 generates
Sharpe > 0.80 with MaxDD < 15%. This captures the Koijen-Moskowitz-Pedersen-
Vrugt (2018) finding that carry predicts returns across all asset classes.

## Mechanism

Carry — the expected return from holding an asset assuming prices stay
constant — is one of the most robust cross-asset predictors in finance:

1. **Koijen et al. (JFE 2018)**: Carry predicts returns across equities
   (dividend yield), bonds (yield-curve slope), FX (interest rate
   differential), and commodities (roll yield). Sharpe ~0.7 per asset
   class, and the cross-asset carry factor has even higher Sharpe due
   to diversification.

2. **Carry proxy via risk-adjusted return**: Without direct access to
   dividend yields, repo rates, and futures curves for all assets, we
   proxy carry with the trailing 63-day return divided by 63-day realized
   vol. This captures the risk-adjusted "expected return" signal: assets
   with high recent risk-adjusted returns tend to continue outperforming
   because: (a) fundamental carry persists (dividends, coupons, roll yield
   change slowly); (b) momentum in risk-adjusted returns is stronger and
   more stable than raw momentum.

3. **Cross-asset rotation**: By ranking across asset classes (not within),
   the strategy captures macro regime shifts. In risk-on regimes, equities
   and EM have high carry; in risk-off regimes, gold and bonds have high
   carry. The ranking automatically rotates without needing an explicit
   regime classifier.

**Why different from H6.4**: H6.4 uses directional momentum z-scores to
classify bull/bear and maps to a fixed risk-on/risk-off basket. H6.5 uses
risk-adjusted return RANKING across assets — no fixed baskets, no
thresholds, just relative carry. The two strategies will agree during
strong regimes but diverge during transitions (H6.5 is more adaptive
because it doesn't require a threshold to be breached).

**Why different from Family 3 (momentum)**: Family 3 TSMOM strategies
use absolute momentum (long or flat based on own trend). H6.5 uses
relative carry ranking (always long top 2 regardless of absolute direction).
These produce different portfolio structures: TSMOM can be fully in cash;
H6.5 is always invested.

## Measurement

- signal: "carry_proxy[i] = return_63d[i] / realized_vol_63d[i] for each asset. Rank 5 assets by carry_proxy."
- entry: "Equal-weight long the top 2 ranked assets (45% each, 10% SHY cash buffer)."
- exit: "Asset drops out of top 2 on next rebalance."
- rebalance: "Monthly (21 trading days). Carry signals are slow-moving."
- universe: "SPY, TLT, GLD, EFA, EEM, SHY (cash)"

## Falsification Criteria

1. Carry-ranked rotation does NOT beat equal-weight 5-asset portfolio (carry ranking has no value)
2. Shuffled carry signals: rotation does not beat random asset selection (p > 0.05)
3. Risk-adjusted return proxy does NOT correlate with fundamental carry measures (proxy is broken)
4. Top-2 strategy does not beat top-1 or top-3 by meaningful margin (N choice is arbitrary)
5. 2022 drawdown > 15% (all assets decline simultaneously — diversification fails)
6. Inverted carry (long bottom 2) performs equally well
7. DSR < 0.95
