# Hypothesis H3.8: Skip-Month Intermediate Cross-Sectional Momentum

hypothesis_id: "H3.8"
family: 3 — Trend Following / TSMOM
created_at: "2026-03-31"
priority: "P1"

## Statement

Ranking 18 ETFs across asset classes by their intermediate-horizon
return from t-252 to t-147 (approximately month -12 to month -7,
skipping the most recent 6 months), going long the top 5 and short
the bottom 5 with equal weight and monthly rebalancing, generates
Sharpe > 0.85 with MaxDD < 15%. This implements Novy-Marx's (2012 JFE)
finding that intermediate momentum (7-12 months ago) is a stronger
and more robust predictor than recent momentum.

## Mechanism

Novy-Marx (2012, Journal of Finance) demonstrated that stock returns
from 7-12 months ago are a significantly stronger predictor of future
returns than recent past returns (1-6 months). The mechanism:

1. **Information diffusion timing**: Fundamental information (earnings
   revisions, macro shifts) takes 7-12 months to fully propagate
   through investor attention and portfolio adjustment. The most recent
   months are contaminated by short-term mean-reversion (Jegadeesh 1990)
   and microstructure noise.
2. **Anchoring decay**: Investors anchor to prices from ~6-12 months ago
   as "reference points" for valuation. Assets that rose significantly
   7-12 months ago are still perceived as expensive by anchored
   investors, creating ongoing underreaction to continued trends.
3. **Cross-sectional vs. time-series**: Unlike H3.5 (skip-month TSMOM,
   which evaluates each asset against its own history), this hypothesis
   uses cross-sectional ranking — relative strength across assets.
   The two signals are distinct: TSMOM asks "is this asset trending?"
   while cross-sectional asks "which assets are trending hardest?"

**Different from H3.5**: H3.5 is time-series momentum with a 1-month
skip. H3.8 is cross-sectional momentum with a 6-month skip, using
intermediate-horizon returns only. Different signal construction,
different skip period, different mechanism (relative vs. absolute).

## Falsification Criteria

1. Intermediate return (t-252 to t-147) has no predictive power vs. random ranking (p > 0.05)
2. Standard 12-month momentum (t-252 to t-21) outperforms — the skip adds no value
3. Long leg and short leg both underperform cash — no cross-sectional spread
4. Inverted signal (long bottom 5, short top 5) is flat, not negative — signal not directional
5. Performance concentrated in one asset class (equities only) — not a cross-asset effect
6. DSR < 0.95
