# Hypothesis H6.1: Real Yield Threshold Rotation

hypothesis_id: "H6.1"
family: 6 — Macro Regime Rotation
created_at: "2026-03-31"
status: "hypothesis_written"

## Statement

When 10-year real yields (proxied by TIP yield = TIP total return minus
inflation breakeven) cross key thresholds, asset class leadership shifts
systematically. Long SPY when the trailing 63-day change in TIP/IEF ratio
is positive (real yields falling = easing), long TLT when trailing 63-day
change is negative AND TIP/IEF ratio is above its 252-day mean (real yields
rising from high levels = tightening peak). This generates Sharpe > 0.80
with MaxDD < 15%.

## Mechanism

Real yields are the price of money after inflation — the single most
important macro variable for cross-asset allocation:

1. **Falling real yields**: Equity risk premium becomes more attractive
   relative to risk-free real returns. Growth stocks outperform as discount
   rates fall. SPY benefits from multiple expansion.

2. **Rising real yields from high levels**: Bond prices fall (TLT declines),
   but when real yields reach restrictive levels (>2%), the economy slows,
   and the next move is toward easing. TLT benefits from the eventual
   rate-cutting cycle.

3. **Proxy construction**: Direct TIPS yield data requires FRED integration.
   The TIP/IEF ratio captures real yield direction: when TIPS outperform
   nominal Treasuries, breakeven inflation is rising (real yields falling
   relative to nominal). This is a tradeable proxy available from yfinance.

**Why different from H6.4**: H6.4 uses price momentum of 4 ETFs as a
macro barometer. H6.1 uses the real yield regime (a fundamental macro
variable) derived from the relative performance of TIPS vs nominal bonds.
Different information source, different rebalancing frequency, different
asset rotation targets.

## Measurement

- signal: "real_yield_proxy = TIP_adj_close / IEF_adj_close (ratio). Direction = sign of 63-day change. Level = current ratio vs 252-day SMA."
- entry_spy: "63-day change in ratio > 0 (real yields falling, easing regime)."
- entry_tlt: "63-day change in ratio < 0 AND ratio > 252-day SMA (tightening from elevated levels)."
- entry_shy: "63-day change in ratio < 0 AND ratio < 252-day SMA (early tightening, ambiguous)."
- rebalance: "Monthly (21 trading days). Macro regimes are slow-moving."
- universe: "SPY, TLT, SHY, TIP (signal only), IEF (signal only)"

## Falsification Criteria

1. TIP/IEF ratio direction has no forward predictive power for SPY vs TLT relative returns (regression p > 0.05)
2. Shuffled ratio series: rotation does not beat static 60/40 (p > 0.05)
3. 2022 regime: strategy holds TLT during rising rates AND TLT drawdown > 15% (proxy fails in rate-hike cycle)
4. Fewer than 15 regime switches in 5 years (too infrequent for statistical significance)
5. Inverted rotation (SPY when tightening, TLT when easing) performs equally well
6. DSR < 0.95
