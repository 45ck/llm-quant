# Hypothesis H13.2: Skew-Weighted Tail Risk Premium

hypothesis_id: "H13.2"
family: 13 -- Skewness
created_at: "2026-03-31"

## Statement

Allocate across 12 liquid ETFs proportional to the absolute value of their
trailing 63-day negative skewness. Assets with more negative skewness (fatter
left tails) earn higher risk premia because investors demand compensation for
downside tail risk. VIX overlay: reduce total equity exposure by 50% when VIX > 25
to avoid tail realization during crisis periods. Expected Sharpe > 0.80 with
MaxDD < 20%.

## Mechanism

Lemperiere, Deremble, Nguyen, Seager, Potters, and Bouchaud (2016, Quantitative
Finance) demonstrated that a "risk premium" strategy -- being long assets that
compensate for bearing risks -- generates significant alpha. The skewness-weighted
variant captures this at the ETF level:

1. **Negative skewness premium**: Assets with more negative skewness (e.g., HYG,
   EEM, XLE) have fatter left tails. Investors rationally demand higher expected
   returns to hold these assets. The premium is persistent because the tail risk
   is real -- it's not mispricing, it's risk compensation.

2. **Skewness as a weighting signal**: Rather than equal-weighting, allocating
   proportional to |negative skewness| overweights the assets with the highest
   tail risk premia. This is economically equivalent to "selling insurance" on
   assets where insurance is most expensive.

3. **VIX overlay for tail protection**: The strategy earns the tail risk premium
   during normal times but must reduce exposure when tails are actually realizing
   (VIX > 25 = elevated probability of tail event). The overlay converts the
   strategy from "naked tail risk selling" to "managed tail risk selling."

**Difference from carry strategies (F9):** Carry harvests the LEVEL of yield/premium.
Skewness weighting targets the SHAPE of the return distribution -- specifically, the
compensation for asymmetric downside risk. An asset can have high carry but low
negative skewness (e.g., short-term bonds) or low carry but high negative skewness
(e.g., EEM). The signals are distinct.

## Falsification Criteria

1. More negatively skewed ETFs do NOT earn higher forward returns (regression slope not significant, p > 0.10)
2. Equal-weight portfolio outperforms skew-weighted portfolio (weighting scheme adds no value)
3. VIX overlay does NOT improve MaxDD by > 5pp (tail protection unnecessary or ineffective)
4. Skewness rankings are unstable month-to-month (the signal is too noisy for monthly rebalancing)
5. Strategy correlation to 60/40 SPY/TLT > 0.60 (just capturing equity + duration beta)
6. DSR < 0.95
