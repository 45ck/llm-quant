# Hypothesis H2.8: XLE/XLI Energy-Industrial Pair Mean-Reversion

## Statement

The XLE/XLI log-ratio (energy sector vs. industrial sector) mean-reverts
when extreme, with a USO 20-day momentum regime filter that suppresses
trades when oil momentum is in the top or bottom 5th percentile (strong
directional oil moves break the mean-reversion). Expected Sharpe
0.80-0.95 with MaxDD < 15%.

## Mechanism

Energy and industrial sectors are economically linked through the
commodity cycle: industrials consume energy as an input cost, so their
relative performance oscillates around a fundamental equilibrium.
The mean-reversion works because:

1. **Input cost / margin cycle**: When oil spikes (XLE outperforms),
   industrial margins compress, creating a temporary divergence that
   reverts as: (a) energy prices stabilize, (b) industrials pass
   through costs, or (c) demand destruction lowers energy prices.
2. **Investor rotation**: Sector fund flows are mean-reverting.
   Momentum chasers pile into the outperforming sector, creating
   overshooting that value-oriented rebalancers correct.
3. **Economic co-movement**: Both sectors are cyclical and correlated
   with global PMI. Divergences reflect transient sector-specific
   shocks (oil supply disruption, capex cycle) that resolve as the
   common business cycle factor reasserts.

**USO momentum filter rationale**: During structural oil regime shifts
(2020 crash, 2022 Russia/Ukraine spike), the XLE/XLI ratio trends
rather than mean-reverts. The USO 20d momentum filter identifies these
trending regimes — when oil is in extreme momentum (top/bottom 5%),
the spread is driven by structural supply/demand shifts, not transient
deviations. Suppressing trades in these regimes avoids fighting
structural trends.

## Measurement

- spread: "log(XLE / XLI)"
- z_score: "(spread - 60d_mean) / 60d_std"
- regime_filter: "USO 20d return percentile rank over 252d. Suppress when rank > 95th or < 5th."
- entry: "Long XLE/short XLI when z < -2.0 AND regime filter passes. Reverse when z > +2.0."
- exit: "|z| < 0.5 OR 20 trading days held (max hold)"
- position_sizing: "2% NAV per trade (Track A constraint)"

## Falsification Criteria

1. XLE/XLI log-ratio does not mean-revert (ADF test p > 0.05)
2. USO regime filter does not improve Sharpe by > 15% vs. unfiltered version
3. 2020 or 2022 drawdown > 15% despite regime filter (filter fails in exactly the regime it was designed for)
4. Shuffled entry signals p-value > 0.05
5. XLE/XLI correlation > 0.80 (too correlated to generate meaningful spread trades)
6. DSR < 0.95
