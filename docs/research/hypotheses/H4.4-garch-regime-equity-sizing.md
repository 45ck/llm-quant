# Hypothesis H4.4: GARCH Regime Filter for Equity Sizing

hypothesis_id: "H4.4"
family: 4 — Volatility Regime Harvesting
created_at: "2026-03-31"
status: "hypothesis_written"

## Statement

A GARCH(1,1) model fitted on SPY daily returns classifies the market into
high-volatility and low-volatility regimes. Reducing equity exposure (SPY)
to 30% when the GARCH conditional variance exceeds its 75th percentile
rolling estimate, and holding 90% in low-vol regimes, generates Sharpe > 0.80
with MaxDD < 15%. The strategy harvests the empirical regularity that
risk-adjusted returns are higher in low-vol regimes while limiting exposure
during vol clustering episodes.

## Mechanism

Volatility clusters (Mandelbrot 1963, Engle 1982). GARCH captures the
autoregressive structure of conditional variance: large returns predict
large returns (of either sign). The mechanism exploits two well-documented
effects:

1. **Low-vol anomaly**: Equities deliver higher Sharpe in low-vol regimes
   because institutional mandates force capital allocation regardless of
   vol level, compressing risk premia in high-vol periods (Baker-Bradley-Wurgler
   2011, Frazzini-Pedersen 2014 BAB factor).

2. **Vol clustering duration**: High-vol regimes persist for weeks-to-months,
   not days. GARCH forecasts mean-revert slowly (alpha + beta ~ 0.98 for
   equity indices), giving the regime switch signal time to add value before
   conditions normalize.

**Why different from H4.1**: H4.1 uses VIX term structure (options-implied,
cross-instrument). H4.4 uses realized-vol GARCH on SPY itself (single-asset,
backward-looking). Different information sources = different signal timing.

**Why different from H4.2**: H4.2 harvests the VRP spread (implied minus
realized). H4.4 uses only realized vol regime classification — no VIX input.
The signals can disagree: VRP can be high even when realized vol is low
(complacent hedging demand), and GARCH vol can spike when VRP compresses
(realized catches up to implied).

## Measurement

- signal: "GARCH(1,1) conditional variance on SPY daily returns. Regime = high if sigma2_t > 75th percentile of trailing 252-day sigma2 distribution."
- entry: "Full allocation (90% SPY) when GARCH regime = low-vol. Reduce to 30% SPY + 60% SHY when regime = high-vol."
- exit: "Regime flip back to low-vol restores full equity allocation."
- rebalance: "Daily regime check, execute on close when regime changes."
- universe: "SPY, SHY"

## Falsification Criteria

1. Shuffled returns test: p-value > 0.05 (GARCH regime has no timing value)
2. Low-vol regime Sharpe is NOT higher than high-vol regime Sharpe (low-vol anomaly absent)
3. GARCH regime switches < 20 in 5 years (signal too slow) or > 200 (signal too noisy)
4. 2022 max drawdown > 15% (fails to de-risk during sustained bear)
5. Inverted signal (overweight high-vol, underweight low-vol) does NOT underperform
6. DSR < 0.95 after multiple-testing correction
