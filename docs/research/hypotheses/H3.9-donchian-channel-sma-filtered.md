# Hypothesis H3.9: Donchian Channel Multi-Asset Trend (SMA-Filtered)

hypothesis_id: "H3.9"
family: 3 — Trend Following / TSMOM
created_at: "2026-03-31"
priority: "P2"
status: "hypothesis_written"

## Statement

A 20-day Donchian channel breakout system across 14 instruments with a
200-day SMA trend filter (only enter breakouts in the direction of the
long-term trend) and ATR-based position sizing generates Sharpe > 0.80
with MaxDD < 15%. The SMA filter suppresses counter-trend breakouts
that are more likely to be false signals.

## Mechanism

Donchian channel breakouts capture the simplest form of trend —
price exceeding its N-day range boundary. The 200-day SMA filter adds
a regime confirmation layer:

1. **Breakout confirmation**: A 20-day high breakout that occurs ABOVE
   the 200-day SMA confirms that the short-term breakout aligns with
   the long-term trend. Breakouts against the long-term trend (price
   makes new 20d high but remains below 200d SMA) are counter-trend
   rallies that frequently fail.
2. **Trend persistence**: The 200-day SMA is the most widely followed
   technical indicator by institutional investors. Its status as
   support/resistance becomes self-fulfilling through behavioral
   coordination — fund managers with "above/below 200-day" allocation
   rules create directional flow that reinforces the trend.
3. **ATR sizing**: Normalizing position sizes by Average True Range
   ensures equal risk contribution across instruments with vastly
   different volatilities (e.g., USO vol ~35% vs. IEF vol ~8%).

**Different from H3.4**: H3.4 uses asymmetric channels (20d entry /
10d exit) without a trend filter. H3.9 uses symmetric 20d channels
with a 200d SMA directional filter. The SMA filter is the key
addition — it should reduce false signals but may also reduce trade
count. If H3.9 outperforms H3.4, the SMA filter adds value; if not,
simpler is better.

## Expected Outcome

- sharpe_range: "0.80 - 0.90"
- max_drawdown: "< 15%"
- trade_count_5yr: "200-400"
- correlation_to_family_1: "0.05" (trend-driven, not credit-driven)

## Measurement

- breakout_signal: "Close > max(high, 20d) → long breakout. Close < min(low, 20d) → short breakout."
- trend_filter: "Long breakouts only when close > SMA(200). Short breakouts only when close < SMA(200)."
- entry: "Enter on next open after confirmed breakout signal"
- exit: "Opposite channel breach: close < min(low, 20d) exits long; close > max(high, 20d) exits short. OR trailing stop at 3x ATR(20)."
- position_sizing: "Risk per trade = 0.5% NAV. Position size = risk / (3 * ATR(20))."
- universe: "SPY, QQQ, IWM, EFA, EEM, TLT, IEF, HYG, GLD, SLV, USO, EURUSD=X, GBPUSD=X, USDJPY=X"

### Parameters

| Parameter | Value | Rationale |
|-----------|-------|-----------|
| channel_period | 20 days | 1 calendar month, Turtle standard |
| sma_filter | 200 days | Institutional long-term trend proxy |
| atr_period | 20 days | Matches channel period |
| trailing_stop | 3x ATR(20) | Wider stop for trend-following |
| risk_per_trade | 0.5% NAV | Conservative per-trade risk |

## Falsification Criteria

1. SMA filter does NOT improve Sharpe by > 10% vs. unfiltered Donchian (filter adds nothing)
2. Shuffled breakout signals p-value > 0.05 (breakout timing is random)
3. Long-only outperforms long/short — short signals have no edge
4. Performance driven by 1-2 instruments — not a multi-asset effect
5. Transaction costs > 20 bps per round-trip destroy profitability
6. DSR < 0.95
