# Hypothesis H2.6: XLE-XLI Energy-Industrial Cointegration Pair

hypothesis_id: "H2.6"
family: 2 — Mean Reversion (Pairs/Ratios)
created_at: "2026-03-31"

## Statement

The XLE/XLI spread (energy sector vs industrial sector) is cointegrated
because energy costs are a primary input to industrial production. A Kalman
filter-adjusted z-score strategy entering at |z| > 2.0 on a 60-day window
and exiting at z = 0 generates Sharpe > 0.80 with MaxDD < 15%.

## Mechanism

Energy (XLE) and industrials (XLI) have a structural cost-linkage:

1. **Energy as industrial input**: Rising energy prices (XLE rallies) compress
   industrial margins (XLI lags). But energy costs eventually pass through to
   product prices, or demand destruction brings energy back down, and the
   spread mean-reverts.

2. **Shared cyclical exposure**: Both sectors co-move with global GDP
   expectations. Their shared cyclical exposure creates a cointegrated
   long-run relationship, while different supply-side dynamics (OPEC, shale
   drilling for energy; capex cycles, reshoring for industrials) create
   temporary dislocations.

3. **Geopolitical shock reversion**: Geopolitical shocks create sharp
   energy/industrial divergence (e.g., Russia/Ukraine 2022: XLE spiked on
   oil embargo, XLI fell on supply chain disruption). These extremes have
   historically reverted within 2-3 months as commodity curves normalize.

4. **Kalman filter advantage**: The hedge ratio between XLE and XLI is
   time-varying — energy's weight in industrial input costs changes with the
   oil price level. A Kalman filter dynamically tracks this relationship,
   reducing tracking error vs static OLS.

**Relationship to H2.5**: H2.5 runs XLE-XLI as ONE of three pairs in a
portfolio. This standalone hypothesis tests whether XLE-XLI alone has
sufficient edge. If it does, the standalone form is simpler to manage.

**Academic backing**: CAIA (2021) pairs trading paper documents sector ETF
pair profitability. The energy-industrial linkage is one of the most
economically intuitive sector pairs.

## Falsification Criteria

1. ADF p-value > 0.10 in >50% of rolling windows (no cointegration)
2. Sharpe < 0.50 after 10bps round-trip transaction costs
3. 2022 energy spike drawdown > 15% (geopolitical dislocation exceeds model)
4. Inverted signal is flat or positive (no directional edge)
5. Shuffled signal test: below 95th percentile
6. Kalman hedge ratio oscillates wildly (unstable relationship)
7. DSR < 0.95
