# Hypothesis H6.2: Cyclical/Defensive Ratio Business Cycle Timing

hypothesis_id: "H6.2"
family: 6 — Macro Regime Rotation
created_at: "2026-03-31"
status: "hypothesis_written"

## Statement

The XLI/XLU ratio (Industrials vs Utilities) is a market-priced proxy for
PMI and business cycle direction. When the 42-day moving average of the
XLI/XLU ratio is trending up (cyclicals outperforming defensives), the
economy is expanding — long SPY. When trending down, the economy is
contracting — rotate to TLT. This generates Sharpe > 0.80 with MaxDD < 15%
by capturing the equity-bond rotation driven by the business cycle.

## Mechanism

The XLI/XLU ratio exploits a durable structural relationship:

1. **Industrials lead the cycle**: XLI (industrials, transports, capital
   goods) is directly exposed to manufacturing activity, capex spending,
   and global trade. When these sectors outperform, it reflects rising
   orders, expanding margins, and improving PMI — all leading indicators
   of broader equity strength.

2. **Utilities are bond proxies**: XLU (utilities) trades as a high-dividend
   duration asset. When utilities outperform industrials, capital is
   rotating from cyclical risk to defensive yield — a classic late-cycle
   or recession signal.

3. **Why this works when PMI itself doesn't**: PMI data is lagged (released
   monthly, with revisions). The XLI/XLU ratio is real-time, market-priced,
   and available daily. It incorporates forward-looking expectations embedded
   in stock prices, not just backward-looking survey data.

4. **Correlation to ISM**: XLI/XLU 42-day trend direction has historically
   matched ISM PMI direction (>50 vs <50) approximately 70% of the time
   with a 2-4 week lead.

**Why different from H6.4**: H6.4 uses a 4-barometer macro momentum composite
(SPY, TLT, DBC, EFA). H6.2 uses a single, focused business-cycle indicator
(XLI/XLU). The narrow focus means H6.2 captures the manufacturing cycle
specifically, while H6.4 captures a blended macro trend. They should agree
during strong trends but diverge during mixed signals (e.g., strong equity
momentum but weak industrial production).

## Measurement

- signal: "ratio = XLI_adj_close / XLU_adj_close. trend = SMA_42(ratio). direction = sign(trend - trend[5d ago])."
- entry_spy: "Trend direction positive (ratio SMA rising) for 3+ consecutive days."
- entry_tlt: "Trend direction negative (ratio SMA falling) for 3+ consecutive days."
- exit: "Direction reversal sustained for 3 days (persistence filter)."
- rebalance: "Weekly (5 trading days)."
- universe: "SPY, TLT, XLI (signal only), XLU (signal only)"

## Falsification Criteria

1. XLI/XLU trend direction has no forward predictive power for SPY returns (regression p > 0.05)
2. Shuffled XLI/XLU ratio: rotation does not beat 60/40 benchmark
3. XLI/XLU direction does NOT correlate with ISM PMI direction at > 60% hit rate
4. 2022 drawdown > 14% (both SPY and TLT fell — rotation doesn't help)
5. Inverted signal (SPY when XLI/XLU falling, TLT when rising) performs equally well
6. DSR < 0.95
