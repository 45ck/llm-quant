# Hypothesis H5.8: Overnight Gap Mean-Reversion

hypothesis_id: "H5.8"
family: 5 — Calendar / Seasonal Effects
created_at: "2026-03-31"
status: "hypothesis_written"

## Statement

When SPY's overnight return (prior close to current open) exceeds 2x
the 20-day ATR, shorting at the open and covering at the close captures
mean-reversion of extreme overnight gaps. The hypothesis: large overnight
gaps are caused by liquidity-thin pre-market overreaction, and the
intraday session partially reverses these gaps. Expected standalone
Sharpe 0.50-0.80 with very low time-in-market (~15-25 trades/year).

## Mechanism

The overnight return anomaly literature (Lou et al. 2019, Barclay &
Hendershott 2003) shows that most equity returns accrue overnight, not
intraday. However, EXTREME overnight returns are different — they
represent pre-market overreaction in thin liquidity:

1. **Liquidity mismatch**: Pre-market trading volume is 5-10% of
   regular session volume. Price discovery with 90% less liquidity
   produces wider bid-ask spreads and overreaction to news.
2. **Institutional rebalancing**: When institutions arrive at 9:30 AM
   and find prices gapped significantly, they often mean-revert toward
   fair value estimates, reversing part of the gap.
3. **Retail herding reversal**: Extreme overnight gaps attract retail
   momentum chasers in the pre-market. Professional flow at the open
   fades this retail-driven overreaction.
4. **Options delta hedging**: Large gaps force options market makers to
   aggressively delta-hedge at the open. If the gap was an overreaction,
   the hedging flow creates mean-reversion pressure.

**This is a CONTRARIAN strategy within Family 5**: Unlike most calendar
effects that exploit directional persistence, this exploits mean-reversion
of extreme events that happen to cluster on specific days (earnings,
macro releases, overnight news). The calendar element is the overnight
session structure itself — a structural feature of equity markets.

## Measurement

- signal: "Overnight return = (Open_t / Close_{t-1}) - 1. Extreme gap = |overnight_return| > 2 * ATR(20)."
- entry: "Short SPY at open when overnight_return > +2*ATR(20). Long SPY at open when overnight_return < -2*ATR(20)."
- exit: "Cover at close same day. Single intraday hold."
- position_sizing: "Fixed size: 2% NAV per trade (max trade size constraint). No pyramiding."

### Parameters

| Parameter | Value | Rationale |
|-----------|-------|-----------|
| gap_threshold | 2x ATR(20) | Filters for genuinely extreme gaps |
| atr_period | 20 days | Standard short-term vol measure |
| hold_period | intraday (open to close) | Mean-reversion is intraday phenomenon |
| max_position | 2% NAV | Risk constraint per trade |
| direction | contrarian (fade the gap) | Mean-reversion of overreaction |

## Falsification Criteria

1. Intraday returns after extreme gaps are NOT mean-reverting — gap continuation is more common than reversal
2. Shuffled gap assignment p-value > 0.05 (no predictive power in gap magnitude)
3. Controlling for earnings/macro release days eliminates the effect — it's event-driven, not gap-driven
4. Symmetric test: upside gaps mean-revert but downside gaps do not (or vice versa) — mechanism is directional, not symmetric
5. The 2x ATR threshold is fragile: 1.5x and 2.5x ATR produce very different results (parameter sensitivity)
6. Transaction costs at bid-ask spread + slippage > mean reversion profit per trade
