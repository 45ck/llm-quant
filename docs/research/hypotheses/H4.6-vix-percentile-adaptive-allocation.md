# Hypothesis H4.6: VIX Percentile Adaptive Allocation

hypothesis_id: "H4.6"
family: 4 — Volatility Regime Harvesting
created_at: "2026-03-31"
status: "hypothesis_written"

## Statement

A continuous VIX-percentile allocation function — mapping VIX's percentile
rank within its trailing 2-year distribution to a smooth equity weight —
generates Sharpe > 0.80 with MaxDD < 15%. Unlike binary regime switches
(H4.1, H4.3), this approach sizes positions proportionally to the vol regime:
full allocation at low percentiles (VIX < 30th pctile), linearly decreasing
to 20% at high percentiles (VIX > 90th pctile), with the remainder in TLT
as a flight-to-quality hedge.

## Mechanism

VIX percentile rank captures the relative fear level compared to recent
history, normalizing for structural VIX level shifts across regimes
(VIX averaged 12 in 2017, 25 in 2020, 17 in 2024). The continuous
allocation function avoids the two failure modes of binary switches:

1. **Whipsaw avoidance**: Binary switches at fixed VIX levels (e.g., 20)
   generate excessive turnover during range-bound vol periods. A continuous
   function changes allocation gradually, reducing transaction costs and
   avoiding "in-out-in" patterns.

2. **VIX level non-stationarity**: A fixed VIX threshold of 20 means
   "moderately elevated" in 2024 but "calm" in March 2020. The percentile
   rank auto-calibrates to the current vol regime.

The TLT allocation during high-vol is not just "parking cash" — it captures
the negative stock-bond correlation during risk-off events (flight to quality).
This transforms a timing strategy into a rotation strategy with positive
carry on both legs.

**Why different from H4.3**: H4.3 uses VIX daily change (spike detection) as
a discrete entry trigger. H4.6 uses VIX level percentile rank as a continuous
sizing function. H4.3 fires on acute spikes (~8 trades in 5 years); H4.6 is
always in the market with varying allocation.

**Why different from H4.4**: H4.4 uses GARCH realized vol on SPY returns.
H4.6 uses VIX (implied vol from options). These diverge when options market
sentiment differs from statistical vol (i.e., most of the time — the VRP
exists precisely because they differ).

## Measurement

- signal: "VIX percentile rank within trailing 504-day (2-year) distribution."
- allocation: "equity_weight = max(0.20, 1.0 - 1.0 * (vix_pctile - 0.30) / 0.60) for pctile in [0.30, 0.90]. Below 30th: 100%. Above 90th: 20%."
- bond_weight: "1.0 - equity_weight (allocated to TLT)"
- rebalance: "Weekly (every 5 trading days). Only rebalance if allocation change > 5% to reduce turnover."
- universe: "SPY, TLT, VIX (signal only)"

### Parameters

| Parameter | Value | Rationale |
|-----------|-------|-----------|
| pctile_window | 504 | 2-year lookback, captures full vol cycle |
| full_equity_pctile | 0.30 | Below 30th pctile = calm, go full risk-on |
| min_equity_pctile | 0.90 | Above 90th pctile = extreme fear, minimal equity |
| min_equity_weight | 0.20 | Never zero equity — VIX extremes often mark bottoms |
| rebalance_threshold | 0.05 | 5% drift required to trigger rebalance |
| rebalance_freq | 5 | Weekly |

## Falsification Criteria

1. Shuffled VIX series: adaptive allocation does not beat static 60/40 SPY/TLT (p > 0.05)
2. Continuous allocation does not improve Sharpe by > 10% vs binary VIX > 20 switch (complexity not justified)
3. TLT allocation during high-VIX periods has negative return (flight-to-quality broken, as in 2022 rate hikes)
4. 2022 drawdown > 13% (stock-bond correlation breakdown)
5. Inverted function (more equity when VIX high, less when VIX low) does NOT underperform
6. DSR < 0.95
