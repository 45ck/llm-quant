# Hypothesis H5.5: Pre-Holiday Equity Drift

hypothesis_id: "H5.5"
family: 5 — Calendar / Seasonal Effects
created_at: "2026-03-31"
status: "hypothesis_written"

## Statement

Holding SPY on the trading day immediately before US market holidays
(10 holidays per year) produces average daily returns 3-10x higher than
unconditional daily returns. A strategy that buys SPY at the open on
pre-holiday days and sells at close generates a positive return series
with annualized Sharpe > 0.50 standalone (noting the very low N of
~10 trades per year requires multi-year evaluation).

## Mechanism

The pre-holiday drift is documented by Ariel (1990), Lakonishok and
Smidt (1988), and confirmed in recent studies (Quantpedia, ScienceDirect).
The mechanism:

1. **Short covering**: Traders reduce short exposure before multi-day
   closures to avoid unhedgeable overnight/weekend gap risk. This
   covering creates buying pressure.
2. **Optimism bias**: Behavioral tendency for positive sentiment before
   holidays. Investors are reluctant to sell into holidays (disposition
   effect amplified by calendar salience).
3. **Reduced selling pressure**: Institutional sellers defer sales
   until after holidays, creating a temporary supply vacuum.
4. **Low volume amplification**: Pre-holiday trading volume is typically
   20-40% below average, making any directional flow have outsized
   price impact.

The effect has been documented across 100+ years of US data and
persists internationally (Cao & Wei 2005). Academic consensus:
mean pre-holiday return is 0.20-0.50% vs. ~0.04% unconditional daily
return — a 5-12x premium for a single day.

## Falsification Criteria

1. Mean pre-holiday return is not significantly positive (sign test p > 0.10 given low N)
2. Pre-holiday returns are not distinguishable from random days (permutation test p > 0.05)
3. Effect disappears in post-2015 subsample (arbitraged away)
4. Post-holiday returns are equally positive — the effect is just market drift, not holiday-specific
5. Low-volume days (non-holiday) show same elevated returns — mechanism is volume, not holiday
