## Rejection Analysis of H4.3-v1

H4.3-v1 used a 20% single-day VIX spike threshold on SPY only with an
open-ended VIX < 20 exit. Result: Sharpe = 1.080, MaxDD = 1.77% — metrics
looked excellent but only 8 trades in 5 years, fatally insufficient for
statistical significance. The signal was real but too rare.

**Root cause**: 20% single-day VIX spikes are tail events (1-2x/year).
Combined with the single-asset (SPY) limitation, the strategy couldn't
generate enough observations.

**v2 changes** (each independently justified, not parameter-tuned to fix v1):
1. Lower spike threshold: 12% (vs 20%) — captures fear acceleration, not just panic
2. Multi-asset: SPY + QQQ + IWM — 3x the opportunity set, same mechanism
3. VIX term structure filter: require backwardation (VIX > VIX3M) — confirms systemic fear
4. RSI(5) oversold confirmation: equity must actually be depressed
5. Shorter exit: 5-day SMA exit + stop-loss (vs open-ended VIX < 20)
6. Extended backtest: 7-10 years (2016-2025) for statistical power

## Statement

When VIX rises >= 12% in a single day AND VIX enters backwardation
(VIX > VIX3M) AND the target equity's RSI(5) < 35, buying the equity
(SPY, QQQ, or IWM — whichever fired the signal) at the close and exiting
when price closes above its 5-day SMA (or 10-day time stop, or -4%
stop-loss) generates Sharpe > 0.80 with MaxDD < 15% over a 7-10 year
backtest with >= 40 trades.

## Mechanism

VIX spikes of 12%+ represent acute fear acceleration — not necessarily
full-blown panic, but a meaningful shift in hedging demand. The mechanism
is the same as H4.3-v1 but the entry threshold captures a broader set of
fear events:

1. **VIX spike (12%+)**: Institutional put demand surging. A 12% VIX jump
   is ~2 sigma for daily VIX changes — statistically unusual but not
   vanishingly rare. This captures: earnings-driven selloffs, geopolitical
   shocks, Fed surprises, and flash crashes.

2. **VIX term structure backwardation (VIX > VIX3M)**: This filter is the
   key innovation. Backwardation means near-term fear exceeds medium-term
   expectations — the market prices an ACUTE event, not a structural regime
   shift. Contango spikes (VIX rises but VIX3M rises more) indicate
   sustained uncertainty and should be avoided. Backwardation has been
   documented as a contrarian buy signal (Bollen-Whaley 2004, Simon-Campasano
   2014).

3. **RSI(5) < 35 equity confirmation**: The equity must be oversold, not
   just options pricing fear. This avoids false signals where VIX spikes
   from low levels during orderly markets (e.g., earnings volatility without
   price decline).

4. **Multi-asset expansion (SPY + QQQ + IWM)**: The mechanism applies to
   all US equity indices — fear spikes in VIX affect the entire equity
   complex. Running across 3 assets triples the opportunity set while the
   VIX signal is a single shared indicator. The assets are correlated
   (~0.85-0.95) so simultaneous entries are effectively one trade with
   diversified execution.

**Why this is NOT HARKing**: The 12% threshold is derived from VIX
distribution statistics (2-sigma daily change), not from optimizing on v1
results. The VIX term structure filter is an independent academic concept.
Multi-asset expansion is a design choice to increase trade count, not a
post-hoc parameter change.

**Why different from H7.1**: H7.1 uses 4 conditions including VIX
percentile rank (90th) and VIX regime multiplier (1.5x SMA). H4.3-v2 uses
VIX TERM STRUCTURE (backwardation) as the regime filter — a fundamentally
different volatility signal. H7.1 is SPY-only; H4.3-v2 is multi-asset.
Expected correlation 0.35-0.50 — related but different entries and exits.

## Falsification Criteria

1. Trade count < 40 across all 3 assets in 7-year development window (still too rare)
2. Shuffled VIX spike dates: strategy Sharpe below 95th percentile of shuffled (no signal)
3. VIX backwardation filter does NOT improve Sharpe vs unfiltered VIX spike alone
4. Inverted signal (sell equity on VIX spike) is flat or profitable (wrong direction)
5. 2020 March drawdown > 15% with all filters active (crisis overwhelms mean-reversion)
6. 2022 drawdown > 10% (sustained bear market with multiple false VIX spike signals)
7. QQQ and IWM add no incremental trades vs SPY alone (multi-asset expansion wasted)
8. RSI filter excludes > 50% of eventual winning trades (too restrictive)
9. DSR < 0.95 after multiple-testing correction
10. Win rate < 55% (insufficient edge per trade for low-frequency strategy)

## 2022 Survival Analysis (Pre-Registered)

- VIX spiked 12%+ multiple times but stayed elevated (no quick mean-reversion)
- Backwardation was intermittent, not sustained
- The RSI + backwardation filter SHOULD have prevented most entries during
  the sustained bear (VIX in contango = no entry)
- If the strategy entered and hit stop-losses repeatedly in 2022, the
  backwardation filter failed and the hypothesis is falsified
