# Hypothesis H3.10: Factor Momentum Long/Short Sector Rotation

hypothesis_id: "H3.10"
family: 3 — Trend Following / TSMOM
created_at: "2026-03-31"
priority: "P1"
status: "hypothesis_written"

## Statement

Ranking 8 sector ETFs by 12-month return excluding the most recent
month (12m-1m, Ehsani-Linnainmaa factor momentum applied at sector
level), going long the top 3 and short the bottom 3 with monthly
rebalancing, generates Sharpe > 0.80 with MaxDD < 18%. The 12m-1m
lookback captures persistent factor momentum while the 1-month skip
removes short-term mean-reversion noise.

## Mechanism

Ehsani and Linnainmaa (2022, Journal of Finance) showed that factor
momentum — the tendency of well-performing factors to continue
performing — explains a large share of individual stock momentum.
Applied to sectors:

1. **Factor exposure persistence**: Sector ETFs have stable factor
   exposures (XLK = growth/momentum, XLE = value/commodity, XLU =
   low-beta/quality). When these factor premia trend, sectors with
   high loading on the trending factor benefit systematically.
2. **Sector rotation as factor rotation**: Going long winning sectors
   and short losing sectors implicitly captures factor momentum
   without needing stock-level factor data. If the momentum factor
   is trending up, XLK (high momentum beta) outperforms XLE (low
   momentum beta), and the long/short portfolio profits.
3. **Skip-month improvement**: Novy-Marx (2012) showed the most
   recent month contains mean-reversion that contaminates momentum
   signals. The 12m-1m lookback removes this contamination.

**Different from H3.6**: H3.6 explicitly computes factor betas and
factor trailing returns to construct a composite factor momentum score.
H3.10 uses simple 12m-1m price returns as a proxy — the idea being
that if factor momentum is the driver, simple sector momentum should
capture it without the complexity of factor decomposition. If H3.6
outperforms H3.10, the explicit factor decomposition adds value.
If not, the simpler model is preferred.

## Falsification Criteria

1. 12m-1m sector return has no predictive power (cross-sectional regression p > 0.05)
2. 12m return without skip outperforms 12m-1m — skip adds nothing at sector level
3. Long leg underperforms SPY — only short leg drives returns (hard to harvest in practice)
4. Inverted signal (long losers, short winners) is not negative — no momentum signal
5. Performance only in bull markets (2023-2025) — no edge in 2022 bear
6. DSR < 0.95
