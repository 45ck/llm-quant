## Statement

A cross-asset carry rotation strategy that ranks 8 diversified assets by their
carry signal (yield or roll-down proxy), goes long the top 4 and avoids the
bottom 4, rebalanced monthly, generates Sharpe > 1.00 with MaxDD < 30%. The
carry signal for each asset class is computed from price-based proxies: momentum
divergence for commodities, duration spread for bonds, dividend yield proxy for
equities, and interest rate differential proxy for FX. This is the highest-ranked
hypothesis in the entire research pipeline due to its exceptional diversification
value (expected rho to F1 ~0.10) and strong academic support.

## Mechanism

Koijen, Moskowitz, Pedersen, and Vrugt (JFE 2018) is the foundational paper:
carry predicts returns in equities, bonds, currencies, and commodities with
portfolio Sharpe ~0.7. AQR Capital Management ($226B AUM) runs carry as one of
four core style premia alongside value, momentum, and defensive. Man Group/AHL
allocates ~25% of its systematic trading to carry strategies.

The cross-asset rotation exploits the fact that carry signals are driven by
different economic fundamentals in each asset class, creating a naturally
diversified portfolio:

- **Commodities (USO, DBA, GLD):** Carry = backwardation/contango regime,
  proxied by short-term vs long-term momentum divergence. Driven by supply/demand
  for physical commodities.
- **Bonds (TLT, IEF):** Carry = yield curve steepness + roll-down, proxied by
  TLT/SHY price ratio momentum. Driven by central bank policy and term premium.
- **Equities (SPY, EEM, EFA):** Carry = earnings yield - risk-free rate, proxied
  by relative price momentum vs SHY (excess return over cash). Driven by equity
  risk premium.
- **FX (EURUSD=X, USDJPY=X):** Carry = interest rate differential, proxied by
  relative momentum vs USD index. Driven by central bank rate differentials.

**The key insight:** When commodity carry is high (backwardation, supply shortage),
equity carry may be low (earnings yields compressed by high rates that cause
backwardation). The rotation naturally tilts toward whichever asset class has
the most attractive carry at any given time, capturing cross-asset diversification
that single-asset carry strategies cannot.

**Why Track B:** The cross-asset rotation has higher expected Sharpe (1.00-1.30)
but also higher MaxDD tolerance (up to 30%) due to the multi-asset long-short
structure. In carry unwind events (2008 GFC, 2020 COVID), all carry positions
can reverse simultaneously, causing correlated drawdowns across the portfolio.
Track B's relaxed MaxDD gate (< 30%) accommodates this tail risk while the
elevated Sharpe gate (>= 1.0) ensures the carry premium is genuine.

## Measurement

### Carry Signal Construction

For each asset, compute a carry score:

```
# Commodity carry (USO, DBA, GLD):
carry_commodity = SMA(price, 21) / SMA(price, 63) - 1.0

# Bond carry (TLT, IEF):
carry_bond = SMA(TLT/SHY, 21) / SMA(TLT/SHY, 63) - 1.0

# Equity carry (SPY, EEM, EFA):
carry_equity = SMA(price/SHY, 21) / SMA(price/SHY, 63) - 1.0

# FX carry (EURUSD=X, USDJPY=X):
carry_fx = SMA(price, 21) / SMA(price, 63) - 1.0
```

### Ranking and Position

- signal: "Rank all 8 assets by carry score, descending"
- entry: "Long top 4 (highest carry), flat bottom 4 (lowest carry)"
- exit: "Monthly rebalance — re-rank and rotate. No intra-month trading."
- rebalance: "Monthly (21 trading days)"
- universe: "USO, DBA, GLD, TLT, IEF, SPY, EEM, EFA (8 assets, 4 asset classes)"
- position_sizing: "Equal weight within the long basket: 7.5% of NAV per position (30% total). Track B allows 15% max per position, but equal weight at 7.5% keeps concentration manageable."
- cash: "Remaining 70% in SHY (cash proxy) — this is a partially invested strategy"

## Falsification Criteria

1. Shuffled carry scores: p-value > 0.05 (ranking by carry has no predictive power for forward returns)
2. Top-4 carry portfolio does not outperform bottom-4 carry portfolio (long-short spread is zero or negative)
3. Cross-asset rotation does not improve Sharpe vs. single-asset carry (H9.1 or H9.2 alone) by > 20% (diversification benefit is illusory)
4. Carry unwind events (2008 GFC, 2020 COVID March): strategy drawdown exceeds 30% (fails Track B MaxDD gate)
5. 2022 drawdown: strategy must demonstrate that rotation away from bonds (negative carry in rate-hiking environment) partially offset losses — if it held TLT through 2022, the carry ranking is miscalibrated
6. Carry-momentum correlation: if rho to F3 > 0.30, the carry signal is just momentum in disguise (not a new mechanism)
7. DSR < 0.95 after multiple-testing correction

## 2022 Survival Assessment

2022 is the make-or-break test:
- Bonds (TLT, IEF): deeply negative carry as Fed hiked rates. Should rank in bottom 4.
- Commodities (USO, DBA): positive carry, energy crisis, backwardation. Should rank in top 4.
- Equities (SPY): negative carry as earnings yield compressed vs rising rates. Should rank low.
- Gold (GLD): mixed — no carry yield, but real rate dynamics. Likely middle rank.

If the carry ranking correctly identified commodities as top carry and bonds as
bottom carry in 2022, the strategy should have been long USO/DBA (which rallied)
and flat TLT/IEF (which crashed). Expected 2022 return: +5% to +15% (commodity
gains offset by flat equity exposure). MaxDD: < 20%.

**Key risk:** The speed of regime changes. The carry ranking uses 21/63 day windows.
If the commodity-to-bond rotation happened too slowly, the strategy may have held
TLT into the early 2022 drawdown before the ranking updated.
