# Hypothesis H5.7: Bitcoin Tuesday Effect

hypothesis_id: "H5.7"
family: 5 — Calendar / Seasonal Effects
created_at: "2026-03-31"
status: "hypothesis_written"

## Statement

Buying BTC-USD at Monday close and selling at Tuesday close captures
a persistent day-of-week effect in cryptocurrency markets. Bitcoin shows
statistically significant positive returns on Tuesdays, driven by
institutional trading patterns and Asian market session overlap.
Expected standalone Sharpe 0.40-0.70 (52 trades/year, noisy but
uncorrelated with equity strategies).

## Mechanism

ScienceDirect (2023, "The Day of the Week Effect in Cryptocurrency")
documented a persistent Tuesday effect in Bitcoin returns. The mechanism:

1. **Asian institutional session**: Asian trading desks (Tokyo, Hong Kong,
   Singapore) begin their week on Monday evening US time (Tuesday Asian
   morning). Capital deployment decisions made over the weekend are
   executed Tuesday morning Asian session, which corresponds to Monday
   night / Tuesday morning UTC.
2. **CME futures roll**: CME Bitcoin futures reference rate is calculated
   daily at 4PM London time. Institutional positions adjustments cluster
   around this window, with Tuesday seeing elevated volumes as weekly
   positioning is established.
3. **Mining payoff cycle**: Large mining operations (historically China,
   now US/Central Asia) sell mined BTC on predictable schedules. If
   Monday is the primary sell day (converting weekend mining proceeds to
   fiat), Tuesday represents post-selling rebound.
4. **Retail pattern**: Weekend retail buying pressure (documented in
   crypto literature) creates demand that clears through exchanges with
   T+1 settlement on Monday, with price impact materializing Tuesday.

**Different from F5-H3 (btc-weekend-drift)**: H5-H3 captures Friday-to-Monday
weekend drift. H5.7 captures Monday-to-Tuesday day-of-week effect —
different calendar window, different mechanism (institutional weekly
start vs. liquidity withdrawal).

## Falsification Criteria

1. Tuesday BTC returns are not significantly different from other weekdays (ANOVA p > 0.05)
2. Tuesday effect disappears post-2023 (structural change from ETF approvals)
3. ETH-USD shows no Tuesday effect — not a crypto-wide phenomenon, likely data artifact
4. Controlling for Monday returns eliminates Tuesday significance — it's autocorrelation, not calendar
5. Effect is driven entirely by 2020-2021 bull market — not present in bearish or sideways regimes
