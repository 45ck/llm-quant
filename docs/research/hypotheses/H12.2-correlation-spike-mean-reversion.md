# Hypothesis H12.2: Correlation Spike Mean-Reversion

hypothesis_id: "H12.2"
family: 12 -- Dispersion
created_at: "2026-03-31"
status: "hypothesis_written"

## Statement

When the average pairwise correlation across 9 SPDR sector ETFs exceeds the 75th
percentile of its trailing 252-day distribution AND is declining (peaked and
starting to normalize), go long the sectors with the lowest individual correlation
to SPY and short the sectors with the highest. Hold 15-30 days. VIX must be < 30
(filter out sustained crises where correlations stay elevated). Expected Sharpe
> 0.70 with MaxDD < 12%.

## Mechanism

Driessen, Maenhout, and Vilkov (2009, JoF) demonstrated that the implied
correlation risk premium is large and positive -- investors overpay for
correlation (index options are expensive relative to constituent options).
When realized correlations spike and then begin to decline, this creates a
mean-reversion opportunity:

1. **Correlation spikes are transient**: Most correlation spikes (except deep
   crises) last 10-30 days. As the panic subsides, correlations revert to
   structural levels. Sectors that were most unfairly dragged down by the
   correlated selloff (low-beta sectors) recover fastest.

2. **Dispersion trade equivalent**: Going long low-correlation sectors and
   short high-correlation sectors is economically equivalent to selling
   correlation -- you profit as correlations decline and sectors differentiate.

3. **VIX < 30 filter**: In true crises (VIX > 30), correlations remain
   elevated for months and the mean-reversion trade gets destroyed. The
   VIX filter eliminates the worst 20% of correlation spikes (the ones that
   DON'T mean-revert) at the cost of missing some quick-reverting spikes.

## Measurement

- signal:
    - "Avg sector correlation = mean pairwise corr(trailing 21d returns) across 9 SPDR sectors"
    - "Correlation percentile = rank of current vs trailing 252d distribution"
    - "Entry condition: percentile > 75th AND declining (5d avg corr < current corr) AND VIX < 30"
- long_leg: "Bottom 3 sectors by trailing 63d beta to SPY (lowest-correlation sectors)"
- short_leg: "Top 3 sectors by trailing 63d beta to SPY (highest-correlation sectors)"
- exit: "15-day time stop OR average correlation drops below 50th percentile"
- position_sizing: "Dollar-neutral, 10% NAV per leg (20% total)"

## Falsification Criteria

1. Declining-from-peak correlation has no forward predictive power for low-beta vs high-beta sector spread (p > 0.10)
2. Random sector selection during same entry windows performs equally well (it's timing, not selection)
3. VIX < 30 filter does NOT improve risk-adjusted returns by > 15% (crisis filter unnecessary)
4. Inverted signal (long high-corr, short low-corr during correlation spikes) performs equally well
5. Trade count < 15 in 5 years (insufficient for statistical significance)
6. DSR < 0.95
