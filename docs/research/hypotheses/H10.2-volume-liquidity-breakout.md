# Hypothesis H10.2: Volume-Liquidity Breakout Signal

hypothesis_id: "H10.2"
family: 10 -- Liquidity
created_at: "2026-03-31"
status: "hypothesis_written"

## Statement

A triple-trigger forced-selling detection system identifies institutional liquidation
events by requiring three simultaneous conditions: (1) daily volume > 2x the 20-day
average, (2) price decline > 1.5x ATR(20), and (3) Amihud illiquidity spike > 1.5x
its 20-day average. When all three fire simultaneously on SPY, QQQ, or IWM, buying
at the next open and holding for 5 trading days generates Sharpe > 0.90 with MaxDD < 15%.

## Mechanism

Forced-selling events create temporary mispricings because the seller's urgency
(margin calls, redemptions, risk limit breaches) overwhelms fundamental valuation:

1. **Volume spike**: Abnormally high volume (>2x average) indicates institutional
   activity, not retail noise. When combined with price decline, it signals forced
   liquidation rather than voluntary rebalancing.

2. **Price decline > 1.5 ATR**: A move exceeding 1.5x the typical daily range
   means the selling is outside normal market dynamics. This filters for genuine
   stress events vs. ordinary down days.

3. **Amihud spike**: When the |return|/volume ratio spikes, it means each unit
   of volume is moving price more than usual -- the market is LESS able to absorb
   the selling. This is the liquidity-specific component that distinguishes this
   hypothesis from simple oversold-bounce signals (H7.1, H7.2).

The conjunction of all three is rare (15-25 events per asset per 5 years) but
high-conviction. The 5-day holding period captures the typical mean-reversion
window as forced sellers finish and value buyers step in.

**Why different from H7.1/H7.2**: Those use VIX/RSI as sentiment indicators.
H10.2 uses volume + price + liquidity -- a microstructural view of actual
market stress, not sentiment proxies. The triple-trigger can fire on calm
VIX days if a single stock/ETF experiences forced selling (e.g., index
rebalancing, margin call cascade in one name).

## Falsification Criteria

1. Triple-trigger events show no forward 5-day return premium vs. random entry (p > 0.05)
2. Single-trigger (volume OR price OR Amihud alone) performs equally well -- triple conjunction adds nothing
3. Shuffled triple-trigger dates produce same Sharpe (p > 0.05)
4. Trade count < 25 total across 3 assets in 5 years (insufficient statistical significance)
5. Mean-reversion does not occur within 5 days -- optimal hold period is > 10 days (different mechanism)
6. DSR < 0.95
