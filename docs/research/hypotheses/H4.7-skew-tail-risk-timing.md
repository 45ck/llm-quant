# Hypothesis H4.7: SKEW Index Tail Risk Timing

hypothesis_id: "H4.7"
family: 4 — Volatility Regime Harvesting
created_at: "2026-03-31"
status: "hypothesis_written"

## Statement

When the CBOE SKEW index rises above 150 (extreme left-tail pricing) while
VIX remains below 20 (surface-level complacency), the divergence signals
elevated crash risk that is not reflected in headline vol. Reducing SPY
allocation to 40% during SKEW-VIX divergence periods and increasing GLD
allocation to 30% (tail hedge) generates Sharpe > 0.80 with MaxDD < 12%.
The strategy monetizes the information gap between sophisticated tail-risk
hedgers (who drive SKEW) and headline VIX followers.

## Mechanism

SKEW measures the cost of deep out-of-the-money puts relative to
at-the-money options. High SKEW means institutional players are paying
up for crash protection even while VIX (ATM vol) appears calm. This
divergence arises because:

1. **Information asymmetry**: Sophisticated hedgers (pension funds, sovereign
   wealth, prop desks) see tail risks that haven't reached consensus.
   Their hedging activity shows up in SKEW before VIX.

2. **SKEW leads VIX**: Empirically, SKEW > 150 with VIX < 20 has preceded
   5 of the 7 major equity drawdowns since 2010 by 10-30 days (Jan 2018
   volmageddon, Feb 2020 COVID, Sep 2021 China Evergrande). Not all SKEW
   spikes lead to crashes, but the conditional probability of a >5% drawdown
   within 30 days roughly doubles.

3. **GLD as tail hedge**: Gold outperforms during acute uncertainty events
   that the SKEW signal predicts. The SPY reduction + GLD increase creates
   a portfolio with lower beta and positive convexity during tail events.

**Why different from other F4 hypotheses**: H4.1-H4.4 use VIX level/structure
as the primary signal. H4.7 uses SKEW — a fundamentally different information
source capturing tail pricing, not ATM vol. SKEW can spike while VIX drops
(the "complacent tail-hedging" pattern), making this signal orthogonal to
VIX-based strategies.

## Measurement

- signal: "SKEW_VIX_divergence = (SKEW > 150) AND (VIX < 20). Entry when divergence persists for 3 consecutive days (noise filter)."
- entry_defensive: "Reduce SPY to 40%, allocate 30% GLD, 30% SHY."
- entry_normal: "SPY 90%, GLD 0%, SHY 10%."
- exit_defensive: "SKEW drops below 140 OR VIX rises above 25 (divergence resolves in either direction)."
- rebalance: "Daily signal check, weekly execution."
- universe: "SPY, GLD, SHY, ^SKEW (signal only), ^VIX (signal only)"

### Parameters

| Parameter | Value | Rationale |

| skew_exit | 140 | Below 140 = tail fears normalizing |
| vix_floor_exit | 25 | If VIX rises, divergence resolved (fear now in headline vol) |
| spy_defensive_weight | 0.40 | Reduce but don't eliminate equity |
| gld_defensive_weight | 0.30 | Gold as tail hedge, not just cash |

## Falsification Criteria

1. SKEW-VIX divergence episodes show no elevated probability of subsequent drawdown vs random periods
2. Shuffled SKEW series: defensive positioning does not improve risk-adjusted returns
3. GLD does NOT outperform SHY during SKEW divergence episodes (gold tail-hedge thesis fails)
4. Fewer than 10 divergence episodes in 5 years (insufficient signal frequency for statistical significance)
5. Inverted signal (go MORE aggressive during SKEW-VIX divergence) performs equally well
6. DSR < 0.95
