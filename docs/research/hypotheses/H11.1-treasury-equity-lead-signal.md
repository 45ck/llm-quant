# Hypothesis H11.1: Treasury-to-Equity Lead Signal

hypothesis_id: "H11.1"
family: 11 -- Microstructure
created_at: "2026-03-31"
status: "hypothesis_written"

## Statement

When TLT's trailing 3-day z-score (return / rolling 20d std) drops below -1.5
(acute Treasury selling), going short SPY and QQQ for 3-5 trading days captures
the equity market's delayed reaction to fixed income stress. Conversely, when
TLT z-score exceeds +1.5 (strong Treasury rally / flight to quality has ended),
going long SPY/QQQ captures the equity recovery. Expected Sharpe > 0.60 with
MaxDD < 15%.

## Mechanism

Lou, Polk, and Skouras (2019, JFE) documented that overnight returns contain
distinct information from intraday returns. The Treasury-to-equity lead extends
this: Treasury markets incorporate macroeconomic information faster than equities
because (1) bond markets have lower noise-trader participation, (2) central bank
communication directly affects rates before equities, (3) institutional fixed
income desks react to macro data releases within minutes while equity market
adjustments take 1-3 days.

The z-score threshold (-1.5 / +1.5) identifies moves outside the normal range,
filtering for genuine macro signals vs. noise. The 3-5 day holding period
captures the typical information diffusion lag from bonds to equities.

**Overlap warning with F1:** This hypothesis uses TLT as a LEADER instrument,
similar to F1 credit lead-lag strategies. However, F1 uses bond LEVELS (trend),
while H11.1 uses bond SHOCKS (z-score extremes). The signal fires at different
times: F1 signals fire continuously during trends; H11.1 fires only on extreme
moves. Expected F1 correlation ~0.15.

## Measurement

- signal: "TLT 3-day return z-score = sum(TLT returns, 3d) / (std(TLT returns, 20d) * sqrt(3))"
- entry_short: "Short SPY + QQQ (equal weight) when z-score < -1.5"
- entry_long: "Long SPY + QQQ (equal weight) when z-score > +1.5"
- exit: "3-day time stop (base), or z-score returns within [-0.5, +0.5], whichever comes first"
- position_sizing: "10% NAV per leg (20% total exposure)"
- universe: "TLT (signal), SPY, QQQ (trade)"

## Falsification Criteria

1. TLT z-score extremes have no forward predictive power for SPY/QQQ 3-day returns (p > 0.10)
2. Inverted signal (buy equity on TLT stress, sell on TLT rally) performs equally well
3. Shuffled z-score series p-value > 0.05
4. Correlation to F1 credit lead-lag > 0.30 (not genuinely different mechanism)
5. Short leg (equity shorts on TLT stress) does not produce positive alpha -- only the long leg works
6. DSR < 0.95
