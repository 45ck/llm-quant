# Hypothesis H5.4: Turn-of-Month Effect (Broad Equities)

hypothesis_id: "H5.4"
family: 5 — Calendar / Seasonal Effects
created_at: "2026-03-31"
status: "hypothesis_written"

## Statement

Holding SPY and QQQ from the last trading day of each month (T-1) through
the third trading day of the following month (T+3) — a 4-day window —
captures >55% of monthly equity returns while being invested only ~19%
of the time. This produces a Sharpe > 0.70 with MaxDD < 12% standalone.

## Mechanism

The turn-of-month (TOM) effect is one of the most persistent calendar
anomalies. Documented by Ariel (1987) and confirmed internationally
by Lakonishok and Smidt (1988), with recent persistence shown by
PMC (2023) and ScienceDirect. The mechanism:

1. **Institutional pension/401k flows**: Monthly payroll contributions
   (typically month-end) create predictable buying pressure on T-1/T+1.
2. **Window dressing**: Fund managers buy winners at month-end for
   holdings disclosure. This demand concentrates in large-cap indices.
3. **Index rebalancing**: Month-end rebalancing by index funds and
   target-date funds creates systematic demand.
4. **Settlement cycles**: T+1 settlement means month-end trades settle
   early in the new month, creating lagged price impact through T+3.

~65% of monthly equity returns are concentrated in the last day + first
3 days (Lakonishok & Smidt, multiple replications). The effect survives
transaction costs because it requires only 24 round-trip trades per year.

**Different from turn-of-month-spy (existing)**: The existing hypothesis
uses a 2-day window (T-1 to T+1). H5.4 extends to the full 4-day window
(T-1 to T+3) per the academic consensus for optimal capture, and adds
QQQ for cross-validation.

## Falsification Criteria

1. TOM window returns are not significantly different from non-TOM returns (t-test p > 0.05)
2. TOM window captures < 40% of monthly return (effect too weak to trade)
3. Sharpe < 0.50 after 24 round-trip transaction costs per year
4. Effect absent in post-2020 data (structural break from passive flow dominance)
5. QQQ TOM effect is not significant — effect is SPY-specific, not systematic
