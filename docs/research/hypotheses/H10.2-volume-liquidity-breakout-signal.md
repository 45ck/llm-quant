## Statement

A triple-trigger signal identifies forced-selling dislocations in liquid
ETFs: (1) daily dollar volume > 2x its 21-day average, AND (2) daily price
decline > 1.5x ATR(14), AND (3) Amihud illiquidity ratio spikes > 1.5x
its 21-day average. When all three conditions fire simultaneously, buying
at the next day's open and holding for 5 trading days captures a mean-
reversion return of 1-3% per event with ~60-65% win rate. Applied across
15 liquid ETFs, this generates Sharpe > 0.90 with MaxDD < 10%.

## Mechanism

The triple trigger identifies a specific market microstructure event —
forced liquidation followed by liquidity reversion:

1. **Volume surge (Trigger 1)**: Abnormal volume (>2x average) in ETFs
   indicates institutional forced selling — margin calls, fund redemptions,
   risk-limit breaches, or index rebalancing. These are PRICE-INSENSITIVE
   sellers who must liquidate regardless of fair value.

2. **Price decline beyond vol (Trigger 2)**: The decline exceeds normal
   volatility (>1.5x ATR), confirming that the selling pressure is
   overwhelming available liquidity. The price has moved beyond what
   fundamental news justifies — the excess decline is a liquidity discount.

3. **Amihud spike (Trigger 3)**: The price impact per dollar of volume
   (Amihud ratio) is elevated, confirming that market-makers have widened
   spreads and reduced depth. This is the microstructure signature of
   temporary illiquidity — and it mean-reverts as market-makers replenish
   inventory and opportunistic buyers step in.

4. **5-day holding period**: Forced-selling dislocations typically resolve
   within 3-5 trading days as: (a) forced sellers complete their
   liquidation; (b) market-makers normalize spreads; (c) value buyers
   absorb the discounted inventory. Holding beyond 5 days exposes the
   position to fundamental risk (the decline might be justified).

**Why this is #2 ranked by diversification value**: The signal fires on
idiosyncratic, event-driven dislocations that are uncorrelated with
systematic factors (credit, macro, vol regime). It is genuinely orthogonal
to Families 1, 4, and 6 because the trigger is microstructure-based
(volume + price impact), not information-based.

## Falsification Criteria

1. Triple-trigger events show no statistically significant positive 5-day forward return vs unconditional (p > 0.05)
2. Win rate < 55% across the 15-instrument universe (signal is noise)
3. Single trigger (volume alone or price alone) performs equally well (triple confirmation adds no value)
4. Shuffled trigger dates: random entry after large-volume days performs equally well
5. Average return per trade < 0.5% after estimated transaction costs (10 bps round-trip)
6. March 2020 drawdown > 10% (multiple simultaneous triggers during systemic crisis overwhelm position sizing)
7. DSR < 0.95

## Robustness Notes

The academic liquidity premium literature (Pastor-Stambaugh, Amihud) studies
individual stocks where liquidity dislocations are larger and more frequent.
ETF implementation concerns:

- **Authorized participant arbitrage**: ETF prices are anchored to NAV by
  AP creation/redemption. Forced-selling dislocations in ETFs may be
  smaller (1-2% vs 5-10% for individual stocks) because APs arbitrage
  the discount within the same day. The 1-3% expected return per trade
  may be optimistic for liquid ETFs like SPY.
- **Correlation spike during crises**: March 2020 saw triple triggers fire
  across 10+ instruments simultaneously. Position sizing limits (2% NAV
  per trade, 5 trades per session) provide hard caps, but the correlation
  of trades within the same stress event could produce aggregate losses.
- **Mitigation**: Cap simultaneous positions at 3 instruments maximum.
  If >3 instruments trigger on the same day, select the 3 with the
  highest Amihud spike (largest dislocation = strongest reversion signal).

## 2022 Stress Test

2022 had several triple-trigger events: Jan 24 (SPY -4%, Ukraine invasion
fears), Jun 13 (SPY -3.9%, CPI shock), Sep 13 (SPY -4.3%, CPI shock).
Expected behavior: each event should show 5-day reversion of 1-3%. Key
risk: the Jun-Sep 2022 period had repeated triggers in a sustained bear
market — individual trades may revert, but the aggregate of 5+ overlapping
positions during a bear could produce net losses. The 3-instrument
simultaneous cap is the primary defense.
