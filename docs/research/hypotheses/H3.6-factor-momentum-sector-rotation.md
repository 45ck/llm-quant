# Hypothesis H3.6: Factor Momentum in Sector ETF Rotation

hypothesis_id: "H3.6"
family: 3 — Trend Following / TSMOM
created_at: "2026-03-31"
status: "hypothesis_written"

## Statement

Ranking 11 sector ETFs by their factor momentum score — the trailing
63-day return of a long/short portfolio formed on each sector's factor
loadings (momentum, value, quality) — and rotating into the top 3
sectors generates Sharpe > 0.80 with MaxDD < 15%. This applies
Ehsani-Linnainmaa (2022) factor momentum to sector ETF rotation.

## Mechanism

Ehsani and Linnainmaa (2022, Journal of Finance) demonstrated that
momentum in factor returns (not individual stock returns) is a powerful
and distinct phenomenon. Factor momentum: factors that performed well
recently continue to perform well. The mechanism is autocorrelation in
factor premia driven by gradual repricing of systematic risk exposures.

Applied to sector ETFs: each sector has different factor exposures
(XLK = high momentum/growth, XLE = high value, XLU = high quality/low
beta). When the momentum factor is trending up, XLK-heavy sectors
benefit. When value is trending, XLE benefits. By ranking sectors on
their implied factor momentum score, we capture this systematic
rotation without needing stock-level factor data.

**Implementation with ETF-only data**: Compute each sector's rolling
beta to SMB, HML, and MOM using SPY as market proxy. The factor
momentum score = weighted sum of (factor_beta * factor_trailing_return).
Factor trailing returns are proxied by:
- MOM proxy: QQQ/SPY 63d relative return (momentum factor)
- HML proxy: XLE/XLK 63d relative return (value factor)
- Quality proxy: XLU/SPY 63d relative return (low-beta quality factor)

## Falsification Criteria

1. Factor momentum score has no forward predictive power for sector returns (regression p > 0.05)
2. Simple 63d price momentum ranking outperforms factor momentum ranking (factor decomposition adds nothing)
3. Shuffled factor returns p-value > 0.05
4. Absolute momentum filter does not improve MaxDD by > 3pp
5. DSR < 0.95 after multiple-testing correction
