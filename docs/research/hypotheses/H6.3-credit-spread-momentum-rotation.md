# Hypothesis H6.3: Credit Spread Momentum Macro Rotation

hypothesis_id: "H6.3"
family: 6 — Macro Regime Rotation
created_at: "2026-03-31"
status: "hypothesis_written"

## Statement

The 21-day rate-of-change of the HYG/LQD ratio (credit spread momentum)
is a leading indicator of equity regime shifts. When the HYG/LQD ratio
momentum is positive (credit conditions improving), go long SPY. When
negative (credit conditions deteriorating), rotate to TLT. This generates
Sharpe > 0.80 with MaxDD < 15%.

## Mechanism

The HYG/LQD ratio captures the credit risk premium in real time — how much
compensation the market demands for high-yield vs investment-grade default
risk. The MOMENTUM of this ratio (not level) is the key signal:

1. **Credit improvement leads equity strength**: Rising HYG/LQD = credit
   spreads tightening = risk appetite improving. This precedes equity
   strength by days-to-weeks because credit markets incorporate information
   from loan officers, CLO managers, and institutional credit desks before
   it reaches equity investors. Collin-Dufresne, Goldstein, Martin (2001)
   established this credit information leadership.

2. **Credit deterioration leads equity weakness**: Falling HYG/LQD = credit
   spreads widening = capital rotating to safety. Corporate bond investors
   are typically more sophisticated and risk-aware than equity retail
   (Baker-Wurgler 2006). Their selling precedes equity drawdowns.

3. **Momentum over level**: The 21-day ROC of HYG/LQD captures the CHANGE
   in credit conditions. The level of the ratio is driven by secular factors
   (interest rate environment, credit cycle position) that are not tradeable.
   The direction-change is the actionable signal — it indicates when regime
   transitions are occurring.

**KEY DIFFERENCE FROM F1 AND F2:**
- F1 credit-lead strategies use credit ETF returns to predict specific
  equity returns (LQD return -> SPY return, 5-day lead-lag).
- F2 H2.3 (HYG-LQD pairs) trades the SPREAD ITSELF for mean-reversion.
- H6.3 uses credit spread MOMENTUM as a MACRO REGIME classifier for
  equity-bond ROTATION. Different timescale (21d momentum vs 5d lead-lag),
  different action (SPY/TLT rotation vs single-equity timing vs spread
  trading).

**Why different from H6.2**: H6.2 uses XLI/XLU (cyclical/defensive equity
ratio) as a PMI proxy. H6.3 uses HYG/LQD (credit quality ratio) as a risk
appetite indicator. Both are macro rotation, but they use different asset
classes as signals (equity sectors vs credit bonds) and capture different
macro dimensions (manufacturing cycle vs credit cycle). Expected correlation
0.25-0.40 — correlated but not redundant.

## Falsification Criteria

1. HYG/LQD 21d ROC direction has no forward predictive power for SPY returns (regression p > 0.05)
2. Shuffled HYG/LQD ratio: rotation does not beat 60/40 benchmark
3. Inverted signal (SPY when credit deteriorating, TLT when improving) performs equally well
4. 2022 drawdown > 14% (both SPY and HYG fell together — no rotation benefit)
5. Correlation to F1 credit-lead > 0.40 (redundant despite different mechanism)
6. Correlation to H6.2 XLI/XLU > 0.50 (both proxying for risk-on/risk-off)
7. Regime switches > 20/year (whipsaw + transaction cost damage)
8. DSR < 0.95
