# Hypothesis H13.1: ETF MAX Effect -- Fade the Lottery

hypothesis_id: "H13.1"
family: 13 -- Skewness
created_at: "2026-03-31"
status: "hypothesis_written"

## Statement

Ranking 18 liquid ETFs by their maximum daily return over the trailing 21 days
(MAX metric), then going long the bottom quintile (lowest MAX) and short the top
quintile (highest MAX) with monthly rebalancing captures the lottery premium.
Assets with recent extreme positive returns are overpriced due to lottery-seeking
behavior. Expected Sharpe > 0.50 with MaxDD < 15%.

## Mechanism

Bali, Cakici, and Whitelaw (2011, JFE) demonstrated that stocks with the highest
maximum daily return (MAX) over the previous month earn significantly lower future
returns -- a "lottery premium" effect. The mechanism:

1. **Lottery preference**: Retail investors overweight assets with recent extreme
   positive returns (lottery-like payoffs), per Barberis-Huang (2008) cumulative
   prospect theory. This demand pushes prices above fair value.

2. **Skewness preference**: Kumar (2009) showed that retail investors systematically
   prefer positively skewed payoffs, paying for the right tail. High-MAX assets
   are the ones exhibiting this positive skewness.

3. **Institutional rebalancing**: After an extreme up day, institutional rebalancers
   mechanically sell (back to target weight), creating selling pressure over the
   following weeks. The high-MAX assets face this selling headwind.

Applied to ETFs rather than individual stocks: the effect should be weaker
(ETFs are less lottery-like than individual stocks) but more liquid and cheaper
to trade. The cross-sectional approach (long low-MAX, short high-MAX) isolates
the MAX premium from market direction.

## Measurement

- signal: "MAX = max(daily_return, trailing 21d) for each ETF"
- ranking: "Rank 18 ETFs by MAX. Long bottom quintile (4 lowest MAX), short top quintile (4 highest MAX)."
- weights: "Equal weight within quintiles. Dollar-neutral."
- rebalance: "Monthly (21 trading days)"
- universe: "SPY, QQQ, IWM, EFA, EEM, TLT, IEF, AGG, HYG, GLD, SLV, USO, DBC, XLK, XLF, XLE, XLV, XLI"

## Falsification Criteria

1. High-MAX ETFs do NOT underperform low-MAX ETFs in forward month (p > 0.10)
2. Long-only low-MAX portfolio does not outperform equal-weight (only short leg drives returns)
3. MAX effect disappears in ETFs -- only present in individual stocks (ETF diversification kills the signal)
4. Shuffled MAX rankings p-value > 0.05
5. Transaction costs > gross long-short spread return (cost-killed with 18 ETFs and monthly turnover)
6. DSR < 0.95
