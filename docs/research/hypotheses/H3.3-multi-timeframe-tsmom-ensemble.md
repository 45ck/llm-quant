# Hypothesis H3.3: Multi-Timeframe TSMOM Ensemble

hypothesis_id: "H3.3"
family: 3 — Trend Following / TSMOM
created_at: "2026-03-31"
status: "hypothesis_written"

## Statement

A multi-timeframe time-series momentum ensemble combining 1-month (21d),
3-month (63d), and 12-month (252d) lookback signals with equal weighting
across 10 liquid asset-class ETFs generates Sharpe > 0.80 with MaxDD < 15%.
Unlike H3.1 (vol-scaled TSMOM with blended signal), this hypothesis treats
each timeframe as an independent sub-strategy that votes on direction —
position is taken only when 2 of 3 timeframes agree (majority vote filter).
This reduces whipsaws from single-horizon noise while preserving multi-scale
trend capture.

## Mechanism

Moskowitz-Ooi-Pedersen (2012) showed TSMOM Sharpe ~1.0 across 58 futures
with 1-12 month lookbacks. Different lookback horizons capture different
behavioral drivers: 21d captures institutional herding and order-flow
persistence, 63d captures macro trend recognition (gradual information
diffusion per Hong-Stein 1999), 252d captures structural regime shifts.

The majority-vote filter is the key differentiation from H3.1's blended
signal approach. Rather than averaging signals (which dilutes strong
single-horizon signals), the vote requires agreement across timescales —
an implicit regime confirmation that reduces false signals in range-bound
markets. Hurst-Ooi-Pedersen (2017) "A Century of Evidence" shows trend
following works across 100+ years precisely because momentum persists at
multiple horizons simultaneously during genuine trends.

**Why different from H3.1**: H3.1 used a continuous blended signal with
vol-scaling and flat thresholds. H3.3 uses discrete majority voting with
no vol-scaling — a fundamentally different signal aggregation mechanism.

## Measurement

- signal: "For each asset: sign(21d ret), sign(63d ret), sign(252d ret). Vote = sum of signs."
- entry: "Long when vote >= 2 (majority bullish). Short when vote <= -2 (majority bearish). Flat when |vote| <= 1."
- exit: "Vote reverses to opposite direction OR vote returns to flat zone."
- rebalance: "Weekly (every 5 trading days)"
- universe: "SPY, QQQ, EFA, EEM, TLT, IEF, GLD, USO, EURUSD=X, USDJPY=X"
- position_sizing: "Equal risk contribution (1/N of 10% vol target per asset)"

## Falsification Criteria

1. Shuffled returns test: p-value > 0.05 (signal has no predictive power)
2. Majority vote does not improve Sharpe by > 15% vs. single-horizon (no benefit from multi-timeframe)
3. 2022 drawdown > 15% (fails crisis alpha thesis)
4. Non-equity sub-portfolio (GLD, USO, FX) Sharpe < 0 (diversifier component fails)
5. DSR < 0.95 after multiple-testing correction
