# Hypothesis H5.6: January Small-Cap Premium (IWM Long / SPY Hedge)

## Statement

Going long IWM and short SPY (equal dollar, beta-neutral hedge) from
December 31 close through January 31 close captures the January effect
in small caps. Tax-loss harvesting selling in December depresses small-cap
prices, creating a January rebound. The hedged spread produces positive
returns in >60% of years with Sharpe > 0.40 standalone (noting 1 trade
per year requires 20+ year evaluation).

## Mechanism

The January effect — small caps outperforming large caps in January —
is one of the oldest documented anomalies (Rozeff & Kinney 1976,
Keim 1983). While weakened since publication, the mechanism persists
because its driver is structural:

1. **Tax-loss harvesting**: Individual investors sell losing positions
   (disproportionately small caps) in December for tax deductions.
   This creates abnormal selling pressure in small caps in December.
2. **January rebound**: As tax-motivated selling ends Dec 31, the
   supply/demand imbalance reverses. New year capital allocations
   (bonus investing, 401k rebalancing) flow disproportionately into
   small caps that were oversold.
3. **Window dressing reversal**: Fund managers who sold small-cap
   losers for year-end window dressing re-enter in January.
4. **Behavioral**: "New year, new portfolio" psychology drives risk
   appetite toward higher-beta small caps.

The SPY hedge removes market beta, isolating the small-cap specific
January premium. This makes the strategy market-neutral — it profits
from IWM outperforming SPY in January, regardless of market direction.

**Weakening concern**: The January effect has weakened since publication
(U Michigan paper, Haugen & Lakonishok 1988). However, the tax-loss
harvesting mechanism is structural (US tax code), and IWM-SPY spread
data shows January still produces positive small-cap premium in ~60%
of years post-2000.

## Measurement

- signal: "Calendar-based. Entry on last trading day of December."
- entry: "Buy IWM, sell SPY at close on Dec 31 (beta-neutral dollar amounts)."
- exit: "Close both legs at close on Jan 31."
- beta_hedge: "Hedge ratio = IWM_beta_to_SPY (rolling 252d). Typically ~1.15-1.25 notional SPY per 1.0 IWM."
- position_sizing: "Long IWM: target notional. Short SPY: target notional * hedge_ratio."

## Falsification Criteria

1. IWM-SPY January spread is not significantly positive (t-test p > 0.10 over 20+ years)
2. January spread is positive but not significantly different from other months — no January-specific premium
3. December tax-loss selling in IWM is not measurable (December IWM returns not significantly negative relative to SPY)
4. Post-2010 January effect is zero or negative — fully arbitraged away
5. The spread correlates > 0.30 with SPY January returns — not truly market-neutral
