# Hypothesis H6.3: Credit Spread Momentum Overlay

hypothesis_id: "H6.3"
family: 6 — Macro Regime Rotation
created_at: "2026-03-31"
status: "hypothesis_written"

## Statement

When HYG (high yield credit) 21-day return is positive AND the HYG/IEF
spread ratio is narrowing (21-day change in HYG/IEF > 0), credit conditions
are improving — hold SPY at full allocation. When HYG 21-day return is
negative AND spread is widening, rotate to TLT. This dual-confirmation
credit momentum signal generates Sharpe > 0.80 with MaxDD < 15%.

## Mechanism

Credit spreads are forward-looking indicators of default risk and economic
health. The HYG/IEF ratio captures the spread between high-yield and
risk-free bonds:

1. **Credit leads equity**: Credit markets incorporate fundamental
   deterioration 2-4 weeks before equity markets because: (a) credit
   analysts focus on cash flow and covenant analysis, which degrades
   before earnings miss consensus; (b) credit markets have more
   institutional participants with fundamental research.

2. **Dual confirmation reduces noise**: HYG return alone is noisy (it
   correlates with rates, not just credit quality). The HYG/IEF ratio
   isolates the credit spread component. Requiring BOTH positive HYG
   momentum AND narrowing spreads creates a higher-conviction signal
   that filters out rate-driven moves.

3. **Situ et al. (SSRN)**: Credit spread momentum — the trend in credit
   spreads — predicts equity returns at the monthly horizon. The effect
   is stronger than credit level, because changes in spreads capture
   the trajectory of credit conditions.

**Why different from Family 1 (credit lead-lag)**: Family 1 strategies
exploit the 1-5 day microstructure lead-lag between credit and equity
prices (information flow). H6.3 uses credit MOMENTUM (21-day trend) as
a macro regime indicator for asset allocation, with monthly holding periods.
Different timescale, different mechanism — microstructure vs. macro regime.

**Why different from H6.4**: H6.4 uses HYG as one of zero macro barometers.
H6.3 uses credit spread momentum as the PRIMARY signal with dual confirmation.
The information overlap is partial (both use HYG data), but the signal
construction and holding period are distinct.

- entry_tlt: "hyg_mom < 0 AND spread_direction < 0 (credit deteriorating on both)."
