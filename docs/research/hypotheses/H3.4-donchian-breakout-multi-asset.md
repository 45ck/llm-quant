# Hypothesis H3.4: Donchian Channel Breakout (Multi-Asset)

hypothesis_id: "H3.4"
family: 3 — Trend Following / TSMOM
created_at: "2026-03-31"

## Statement

A 20-day Donchian channel breakout system across 8 liquid ETFs (equities,
bonds, commodities, FX) generates Sharpe > 0.80 with MaxDD < 15%. Entry
on new 20-day high close, exit on 10-day low close (asymmetric channels —
faster exit than entry). ATR-based position sizing normalizes risk
contribution across asset classes with different volatilities.

## Mechanism

Donchian channels capture the simplest form of trend — a new N-day high
indicates a price breakout from its recent range. The mechanism works
because: (1) breakouts from congestion zones trigger stop-loss cascading
and trend-following order flow, (2) new highs attract momentum chasers
who amplify the trend, (3) institutional rebalancing flows persist for
days after regime shifts.

The 20-day/10-day asymmetric setup (Turtle Traders variant) is
theoretically motivated: trends develop slowly (need 20 days to confirm)
but fail fast (10-day exit captures reversals quickly). This asymmetry
reduces time spent in losing positions vs. symmetric channels.

**Why fewer parameters matter**: Donchian has only 2 parameters (entry
channel, exit channel) vs. TSMOM's 5+ (lookback, blend weights, vol
window, vol target, thresholds). Fewer parameters = less overfitting
surface. If the result is comparable to H3.1/H3.3, the simpler model
is preferred (Occam's razor / minimum description length).

## Measurement

- signal: "Close > max(close, 20d) → entry signal. Close < min(close, 10d) → exit signal."
- entry: "Long on close when new 20-day high. No shorts (long-only variant)."
- exit: "Sell on close when new 10-day low OR trailing stop at 2x ATR(20) from peak."
- rebalance: "Daily signal check, act on next open"
- universe: "SPY, QQQ, EFA, TLT, GLD, USO, EURUSD=X, GBPUSD=X"
- position_sizing: "ATR-normalized: target_risk / ATR(20) per asset. Target risk = 0.5% NAV per position per day."

### Parameters

| entry_channel | 20 days | Turtle Traders default, 1 calendar month |
| exit_channel | 10 days | Faster exit, asymmetric by design |
| atr_period | 20 days | Matches entry channel |
| trailing_stop | 2x ATR(20) | Standard risk management |

## Falsification Criteria

1. Sharpe < 0.60 across full backtest period (no trend edge)
2. Shuffled returns p-value > 0.05 (breakout timing has no value)
3. Symmetric channels (20/20) outperform asymmetric (20/10) — contradicts fast-exit thesis
4. Performance driven by single asset (SPY or GLD) — not a multi-asset effect
5. DSR < 0.95 after multiple-testing correction
