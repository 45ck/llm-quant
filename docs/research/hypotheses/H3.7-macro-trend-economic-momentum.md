# Hypothesis H3.7: Economic/Macro Trend Momentum (Brooks-Feilbogen Inspired)

## Statement

A macro trend momentum strategy using 3 observable price-based proxies
for economic momentum — real yield trend (TIP vs IEF relative return),
credit spread trend (LQD vs IEF relative return), and commodity cycle
trend (DBC 63d momentum) — to rotate between risk-on (SPY+EEM+HYG),
risk-off (TLT+GLD+SHY), and neutral (60/40 SPY/TLT) baskets generates
Sharpe > 0.80 with MaxDD < 15%.

## Mechanism

Brooks and Feilbogen (AQR 2024) demonstrated that economic momentum —
trends in macro indicators like real yields, credit conditions, and
commodity cycles — predicts asset returns with Sharpe 0.5-0.7 per theme
and low cross-theme correlation. The critical insight: macro trends
persist because economic regimes are sticky (central bank policy moves
slowly, business cycles span years, not weeks).

**Price-based proxy advantage**: Rather than using lagging economic data
(PMI published monthly with revisions, GDP quarterly), we use ETF-based
proxies that embed real-time market consensus:
- **Real yield trend**: TIP/IEF relative return — when TIPS outperform
  nominals, real yields are falling (expansionary). Rising = contractionary.
- **Credit spread trend**: LQD/IEF relative return — when IG credit
  outperforms Treasuries, credit conditions are easing. Widening = tightening.
- **Commodity cycle**: DBC 63d return — positive = inflationary/growth
  impulse. Negative = disinflationary/recessionary.

Composite score = equal-weight z-score of three trends. This differs from
H6.4 (macro-momentum-barometer) which uses SPY/TLT/DBC/EFA as barometers
for regime classification. H3.7 uses economic relationship proxies (real
yields, credit spreads) rather than raw asset momentum.

## Measurement

- trend_signals:
    - real_yield_trend: "63d return of TIP / IEF. Z-score against 252d history."
    - credit_trend: "63d return of LQD / IEF. Z-score against 252d history."
    - commodity_trend: "63d return of DBC. Z-score against 252d history."
- composite: "Equal-weight average of 3 z-scores."
- regimes:
    - risk_on: "composite > +0.5 → SPY 40%, EEM 20%, HYG 20%, GLD 10%, TLT 10%"
    - neutral: "composite between -0.5 and +0.5 → SPY 40%, TLT 30%, GLD 15%, SHY 15%"
    - risk_off: "composite < -0.5 → TLT 35%, GLD 25%, SHY 30%, SPY 10%"
- rebalance: "Weekly (every 5 trading days), act on composite signal"

## Falsification Criteria

1. Composite z-score has no forward predictive power for risk-on vs. risk-off basket (p > 0.05)
2. Single-theme strategies (any one of 3 trends) outperform the composite — diversification across themes adds no value
3. 2022 drawdown > 15% (macro momentum should have been risk-off in 2022)
4. Random regime assignment performs comparably (shuffled signal test)
5. Credit spread trend is redundant with Family 1 credit lead-lag (correlation > 0.50)
6. DSR < 0.95 after multiple-testing correction
