# Hypothesis H4.8: Sector Implied-Realized Vol Spread Rotation

hypothesis_id: "H4.8"
family: 4 — Volatility Regime Harvesting
created_at: "2026-03-31"
status: "hypothesis_written"

## Statement

Ranking sector ETFs by their implied-minus-realized volatility spread
(IV-RV) and going long the top 3 sectors with the highest IV-RV (richest
options premium = highest fear) while underweighting sectors with negative
IV-RV (cheapest options = complacency) generates Sharpe > 0.80 with
MaxDD < 15%. Monthly rebalancing. The strategy harvests the variance risk
premium at the sector level, where it is less crowded than at the index level.

## Mechanism

The variance risk premium (VRP) — the systematic overpricing of implied
volatility relative to subsequently realized volatility — is one of the most
well-documented anomalies in finance:

1. **Sector VRP dispersion**: At the index level, VRP harvesting is crowded
   (DeStefano 2016, Carr-Wu 2009). At the sector level, options are less
   liquid and institutional hedging is less systematic, creating wider and
   more exploitable IV-RV spreads.

2. **Fear as a signal**: Sectors with the highest IV-RV spread have the most
   embedded fear premium. This fear is compensated — investors who bear the
   risk of holding high-IV sectors earn the premium when realized vol comes
   in below implied (which happens ~80% of the time per Bollerslev 2009).

3. **No direct options exposure**: The strategy does NOT sell options. It
   uses IV-RV as a SIGNAL for sector equity rotation. Going long the equity
   ETF in sectors where fear is highest captures the mean-reversion of that
   fear without taking gamma/vega risk.

4. **Sector-specific events**: Earnings seasons, regulatory actions, and
   commodity shocks create sector-specific IV spikes (e.g., XLE during oil
   volatility, XLF during banking stress). These spikes systematically
   overshoot realized outcomes, creating rotation alpha.

**Why different from H4.5**: H4.5 uses cross-sector REALIZED vol dispersion
as a SPY timing signal. H4.8 uses per-sector IV-MINUS-RV for sector
ROTATION. Different signal, different action, different mechanism.

**Why different from H7.7**: H7.7 (correlation risk premium) harvests the
implied-realized CORRELATION spread across sectors. H4.8 harvests the
implied-realized VOLATILITY spread within individual sectors. Correlation
and volatility are related but distinct quantities.

## Falsification Criteria

1. Sector IV-RV ranking has no forward predictive power for sector returns (IC < 0.02)
2. Shuffled IV-RV rankings produce similar Sharpe (no signal)
3. IV-RV spread is uniform across sectors (no exploitable dispersion)
4. Top IV-RV sectors UNDERPERFORM bottom sectors (wrong sign)
5. March 2020 drawdown > 15% (IV spike fails to predict recovery)
6. DSR < 0.95
7. Options data quality insufficient for reliable IV estimation
