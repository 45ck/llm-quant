# Hypothesis H4.5: Sector Implied-Realized Vol Dispersion

hypothesis_id: "H4.5"
family: 4 — Volatility Regime Harvesting
created_at: "2026-03-31"
status: "hypothesis_written"

## Statement

When cross-sector realized volatility dispersion (standard deviation of
trailing 20-day realized vol across 9 SPDR sector ETFs) spikes above its
80th percentile, subsequent 10-day SPY returns are positive as dispersion
mean-reverts. Going long SPY when sector vol dispersion > 80th percentile
and exiting when dispersion returns below the 50th percentile generates
Sharpe > 0.80 with MaxDD < 15%.

## Mechanism

Sector vol dispersion measures disagreement in risk pricing across the
economy. Extreme dispersion occurs when one or two sectors experience
idiosyncratic stress (e.g., energy crisis, banking scare) while others
remain calm. This creates a mean-reversion opportunity because:

1. **Rotation flows**: When one sector's vol spikes, portfolio rebalancers
   sell the stressed sector and buy calm sectors. This cross-sector flow
   compresses dispersion and supports broad-market prices.

2. **Contagion overshoot**: Markets initially overestimate contagion risk
   from sector-specific shocks (Bekaert-Harvey-Ng 2005). When the stress
   turns out to be contained, the broad index recovers.

3. **Vol of vol is mean-reverting**: Dispersion in sector vols is a
   second-moment effect that mean-reverts faster than first-moment
   (price) effects. Bollerslev-Todorov-Xu (2015) show variance-of-variance
   is a priced risk factor with positive expected returns.

**Why different from H4.1/H4.2/H4.3**: Those hypotheses use VIX (options
market, aggregate) as the signal. H4.5 uses realized vol dispersion across
sectors (equity-only, cross-sectional). The signal fires at different times:
VIX can be elevated with uniform sector stress (no dispersion spike), and
sector dispersion can spike while VIX stays moderate (contained sector shock).

## Measurement

- signal: "Dispersion = std_dev(RV_20d) across [XLK, XLF, XLE, XLV, XLI, XLY, XLP, XLU, XLB]. Percentile rank vs trailing 252-day distribution."
- entry: "Long SPY at 90% weight when dispersion percentile > 80th."
- exit: "Dispersion returns below 50th percentile OR 20-day time stop."
- rebalance: "Daily signal check, weekly execution (avoid overtrading)."
- universe: "SPY, XLK, XLF, XLE, XLV, XLI, XLY, XLP, XLU, XLB, SHY"

## Falsification Criteria

1. Shuffled sector returns: dispersion-timed entry does not beat random entry (p > 0.05)
2. Dispersion has no forward predictive power for SPY 10-day returns (regression slope not significant)
3. Equal-weight sector basket outperforms SPY timing (the alpha is in sector selection, not timing)
4. 2020 March and 2022 bear: both produce drawdowns > 12% (mechanism fails in broad crises)
5. Inverted signal (buy when dispersion is LOW) performs equally well
6. DSR < 0.95
