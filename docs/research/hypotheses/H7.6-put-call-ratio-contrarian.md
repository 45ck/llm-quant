## Statement

When the CBOE equity put/call ratio z-score exceeds +2.0 (calculated
against a 60-day lookback), extreme bearish hedging demand signals
an imminent reversal. Go long SPY and hold until the z-score returns
below +0.5. This contrarian signal fires 5-10 times per year and
captures mean-reversion of sentiment extremes. Expected Sharpe
0.50-0.70 standalone.

## Mechanism

The equity put/call ratio measures aggregate demand for downside
protection relative to upside speculation. At extremes:

1. **Bearish extremes (high P/C) signal bottoms**: When the P/C
   z-score > 2.0, institutions and retail are aggressively buying
   puts. This indicates maximum bearishness — the crowd is already
   positioned for decline. From a positioning perspective, further
   downside requires NEW sellers, but maximum bearishness means
   most sellers have already sold. The asymmetry favors upside.
2. **Options market maker hedging**: When put buying spikes, market
   makers accumulate short delta. They hedge by buying the
   underlying, creating mechanical buying pressure that supports
   prices and eventually drives the reversal.
3. **Mean-reversion of fear**: Behavioral finance (Kahneman, Tversky)
   shows that investors overweight tail risks after recent volatility.
   P/C extremes coincide with recency bias peaks — the fear is
   greatest just as the risk is dissipating.
4. **60-day z-score normalization**: Absolute P/C levels drift over
   time (structural shift from options market growth). Z-score
   normalization against recent history captures relative extremes
   regardless of absolute level.

## Measurement

- signal: "CBOE equity P/C ratio 5-day SMA (smooth daily noise)"
- z_score: "(P/C 5d SMA - 60d mean) / 60d std"
- entry: "Long SPY when z_score > +2.0"
- exit: "z_score < +0.5 OR 20 trading days held (max hold)"
- position_sizing: "2% NAV per trade (Track A)"

### Parameters

| Parameter | Value | Rationale |

| pc_smoothing | 5-day SMA | Smooth daily options noise |

| z_entry | 2.0 | 2-sigma extreme bearishness |
| z_exit | 0.5 | Return toward normal sentiment |
| max_hold | 20 days | ~4 weeks — sentiment reverts within this window |

## Data Requirements

- CBOE equity put/call ratio daily (from CBOE website or paid data feed)
- NOT available from yfinance — requires external data integration
- Alternative: compute from SPY options volume if options data is available

## Falsification Criteria

1. High P/C z-score does not predict positive SPY returns (regression p > 0.05)
2. Shuffled P/C signal p-value > 0.05 (timing has no value)
3. Bullish extremes (low P/C, z < -2.0) do not predict negative SPY returns — signal is one-sided
4. VIX > 25 conditioning eliminates the effect — it's just buying VIX spikes
5. Post-2020 effect disappears (0DTE options explosion changed P/C dynamics)
6. DSR < 0.95
