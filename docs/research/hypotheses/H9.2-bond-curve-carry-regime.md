# Hypothesis H9.2: Bond Curve Carry with Regime Filter

## Statement

A bond carry strategy that goes long TLT when the yield curve is steep (high
roll-down return) and rotates to SHY when the curve is flat or inverted generates
Sharpe > 0.70 with MaxDD < 15%. The signal is the TLT-SHY spread: when long-duration
bonds offer substantially higher carry than short-duration bonds, the curve is steep
enough to compensate for duration risk. A regime filter (rate stability measured by
21-day rolling volatility of TLT returns) prevents entry during rate shock periods
where carry is overwhelmed by capital losses.

## Mechanism

Bond carry comes from two sources:

1. **Yield carry:** Coupon income from holding the bond. TLT (20+ year duration) has
   higher yield than SHY (1-3 year duration) when the curve is steep. This is the
   basic term premium.

2. **Roll-down return:** As time passes, a 20-year bond becomes a 19-year bond, rolling
   down the yield curve to lower yields (higher prices) in a steep curve environment.
   Roll-down can add 50-200 bps annualized to TLT returns when the curve is steep.
   When the curve is flat, roll-down is near zero.

The TLT-SHY price ratio captures the combined yield + roll-down advantage of
long-duration bonds. A rising ratio means long duration is outperforming (steep
curve, positive carry). A falling ratio means short duration is winning (flat/inverted
curve, carry compressed or negative).

**The regime filter is critical:** 2022 demonstrated that carry can be overwhelmed by
rate shocks. TLT fell ~30% as the Fed raised rates 425 bps. The carry from the steep
curve was trivial compared to the capital loss from rising rates. The regime filter
uses 21-day rolling volatility of TLT returns — when TLT vol exceeds its 75th
percentile (rate instability), the strategy rotates to SHY regardless of curve
steepness.

**Why this is NOT F6 (macro regime rotation):** F6 uses yield curve slope as one of
several macro indicators (alongside VIX, PMI, etc.) to determine broad asset
allocation. This strategy uses *bond-specific carry* as the signal — the yield
differential between long and short duration — and only rotates within the fixed
income sleeve. It is a duration timing strategy, not a macro regime strategy.

## Measurement

- signal: "curve_carry = (TLT_price / SHY_price) normalized to z-score over 63-day rolling window"
- regime_filter: "tlt_vol_21d = rolling_std(TLT daily returns, 21) * sqrt(252). If tlt_vol_21d > percentile_75(tlt_vol_21d, 252d lookback), regime = 'unstable' -> SHY"
- entry: "Long TLT when curve_carry z-score > +0.5 AND regime = 'stable'"
- exit: "Rotate to SHY when curve_carry z-score < -0.5 OR regime = 'unstable'"
- rebalance: "Weekly (every 5 trading days)"
- universe: "TLT (long duration), IEF (intermediate — optional), SHY (safe haven rotation target)"
- position_sizing: "Max 10% of NAV in TLT position (Track A limit)"

## Falsification Criteria

1. Shuffled returns test: p-value > 0.05 (curve_carry signal has no predictive power)
2. Strategy returns during "steep curve + stable rates" periods are not significantly > SHY returns (carry mechanism not operating)
3. Regime filter does not improve risk-adjusted returns vs. unfiltered version (vol filter adds no value)
4. 2022 performance: strategy must have rotated to SHY during the rate shock period (Q1-Q3 2022). If it held TLT through the 30% drawdown, the regime filter failed.
5. Inverted curve periods (2023): strategy must have been in SHY. If it was long TLT when curve was inverted, the carry signal is miscalibrated.
6. DSR < 0.95 after multiple-testing correction

## 2022 Survival Assessment

Critical test year. TLT fell ~30% in 2022 as rates spiked. The regime filter
(TLT vol > 75th percentile) should have triggered by mid-Q1 2022 as rate
volatility surged, rotating the strategy to SHY before the worst of the drawdown.
If the filter calibration is correct, the strategy should have avoided most of
the TLT drawdown. However: the vol spike was fast — if the 21-day window is too
slow, the filter may have triggered after significant losses. The exact timing
of the filter trigger vs. the drawdown onset is a key backtest question.

Expected 2022 drawdown: < 10% (compared to TLT's -30%) if regime filter works.
