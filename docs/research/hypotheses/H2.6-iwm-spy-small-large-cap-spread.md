# Hypothesis H2.6: IWM/SPY Small-Large Cap Spread Mean-Reversion

## Statement

The IWM/SPY spread (small-cap vs. large-cap) mean-reverts on a 60-day
horizon. Using a 60-day OLS hedge ratio to construct a beta-neutral
spread, entering when the z-score exceeds +/-2.0 standard deviations
and exiting at +/-0.5, with a 25-day maximum holding period and a stop
at |z|>3.5, generates Sharpe > 0.85 with MaxDD < 15%.

## Mechanism

The small-cap / large-cap spread reflects relative risk appetite. When
the spread deviates significantly from its OLS-fitted equilibrium, it
tends to revert because:

1. **Institutional rebalancing**: Target-weight portfolio managers
   (pension funds, balanced funds) mechanically rebalance between
   large-cap and small-cap allocations when drift exceeds tolerance
   bands. This creates mean-reverting flow.
2. **Risk-appetite mean-reversion**: Extreme IWM outperformance signals
   euphoric risk-seeking (unsustainable); extreme SPY outperformance
   signals excessive flight-to-quality (overcorrection). Both states
   revert as sentiment normalizes.
3. **Factor rotation**: The IWM/SPY spread proxies the size factor
   (SMB). Factor returns exhibit short-term mean-reversion (Asness
   et al. 2013) even though they trend at intermediate horizons.

The 60-day OLS hedge ratio adapts to changing beta relationships
(IWM beta to SPY varies from 1.1 to 1.4 depending on vol regime).
The z-score normalization accounts for time-varying spread volatility.

## Measurement

- spread: "IWM - hedge_ratio * SPY, where hedge_ratio = OLS beta from rolling 60d regression"
- z_score: "(spread - 60d_mean) / 60d_std"
- entry: "Long spread when z < -2.0, short spread when z > +2.0"
- exit: "|z| < 0.5 (mean-reversion target) OR 25 trading days held (max hold) OR |z| > 3.5 (stop-loss)"
- position_sizing: "2% NAV per trade (Track A constraint)"

### Parameters

| Parameter | Value | Rationale |
|-----------|-------|-----------|
| hedge_ratio_window | 60 days | 3-month OLS regression |
| z_entry | 2.0 std | 2-sigma deviation — statistically extreme |
| z_exit | 0.5 std | Near mean — partial reversion target |
| z_stop | 3.5 std | Spread dislocation — structural break risk |
| max_hold | 25 trading days | ~5 weeks max — prevents indefinite exposure |

## Falsification Criteria

1. Spread z-score has no mean-reverting properties (unit root test p > 0.05)
2. Shuffled entry signals p-value > 0.05 (timing has no value)
3. OLS hedge ratio is unstable — residual autocorrelation changes sign across regimes
4. Max holding period is reached on >40% of trades (spread not reverting fast enough)
5. Stop-loss triggered on >20% of trades (spread dislocation is structural, not temporary)
6. DSR < 0.95
