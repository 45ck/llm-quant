# Hypothesis H2.7: HYG/LQD Credit Quality Pair Mean-Reversion

hypothesis_id: "H2.7"
family: 2 — Mean Reversion (Pairs/Ratios)
created_at: "2026-03-31"
track: "B"
priority: "P2"
status: "hypothesis_written"

## Statement

The HYG/LQD credit quality spread — high-yield vs. investment-grade
corporate bonds — mean-reverts after controlling for duration using IEF
as a proxy. A 60-day regression of HYG on LQD and IEF produces a
duration-neutralized residual. When the residual z-score exceeds
+/-1.8, entering and exiting at +/-0.3 generates Sharpe > 0.90 with
MaxDD < 20% (Track B tolerance).

## Mechanism

The HYG-LQD spread isolates the pure credit risk premium — the
compensation for default risk net of interest rate sensitivity. This
spread mean-reverts because:

1. **Credit cycle mean-reversion**: Credit spreads widen during risk-off
   and compress during risk-on. Extreme widening (HYG underperforms LQD)
   is typically followed by compression as default fears prove
   exaggerated. Extreme compression is followed by widening as
   complacency corrects.
2. **Duration contamination removal**: Raw HYG/LQD ratios mix credit
   and duration effects. By regressing out IEF (7-10yr Treasury),
   the residual captures pure credit quality rotation, not rate moves.
3. **Institutional flow dynamics**: High-yield fund flows are
   mean-reverting — retail investors chase HYG after spread compression
   and panic-sell after widening, creating overshooting in both
   directions that institutional capital corrects.

**Caveat — Family 1 correlation**: This hypothesis has estimated
correlation 0.35 to Family 1 credit lead-lag strategies because both
trade on credit market signals. However, the mechanism is different:
Family 1 uses credit→equity lead-lag (directional), while H2.7 uses
credit quality spread mean-reversion (non-directional). The
correlation is driven by shared credit regime sensitivity, not
redundant signals.

## Measurement

- regression: "HYG = alpha + beta1*LQD + beta2*IEF + residual (rolling 60d OLS)"
- z_score: "(residual - 60d_mean) / 60d_std"
- entry: "Long HYG/short LQD when z < -1.8. Short HYG/long LQD when z > +1.8."
- exit: "|z| < 0.3 (mean-reversion target)"
- duration_hedge: "IEF position sized to neutralize residual duration exposure"
- position_sizing: "3% NAV per trade (Track B constraint)"

### Parameters

| Parameter | Value | Rationale |
|-----------|-------|-----------|
| regression_window | 60 days | 3-month rolling OLS |
| duration_proxy | IEF | 7-10yr Treasury — intermediate duration control |
| z_entry | 1.8 std | Slightly lower than H2.6 — credit spreads are noisier |
| z_exit | 0.3 std | Near-zero mean-reversion target |
| max_hold | 30 trading days | Credit spreads revert slower than equity pairs |

## Falsification Criteria

1. Duration-neutralized residual does not mean-revert (ADF test p > 0.05)
2. Removing IEF duration control does not degrade performance — duration proxy is unnecessary
3. Correlation to Family 1 strategies > 0.50 — insufficiently independent for portfolio
4. Shuffled entry signals p-value > 0.05
5. 2020 March drawdown > 25% (credit dislocation overwhelms the strategy)
6. DSR < 0.95
