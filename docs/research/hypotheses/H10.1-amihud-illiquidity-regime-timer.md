## Statement

When a market-wide Amihud illiquidity ratio (|daily return| / daily dollar
volume, averaged across SPY, QQQ, IWM, EFA, EEM) is declining on a 21-day
trend basis (liquidity expanding), small-cap equities (IWM) outperform
large-cap (SPY) as capital flows into less liquid corners of the market.
When the Amihud ratio is rising (liquidity contracting), rotate to SPY or
SHY. A combined signal of -1 x Amihud_Regime + Volume_Trend with a 3-day
confirmation buffer generates Sharpe > 0.80 with MaxDD < 15%.

## Mechanism

The liquidity premium is one of the most robust anomalies in asset pricing:

1. **Pastor-Stambaugh (2003, JPE)**: Stocks with high sensitivity to
   aggregate liquidity shocks earn 7.5% more annually. Their liquidity
   factor carries a Fama-French alpha of 9.23%/yr (t=4.29). This is not
   a size effect or value effect — it is a pure liquidity risk premium.

2. **Amihud (2002)**: The illiquidity ratio (|r|/volume) is the simplest
   and most widely used liquidity measure. When aggregate Amihud rises,
   market-makers widen spreads, institutional flow dries up, and less
   liquid assets suffer disproportionately. When Amihud declines, liquidity
   returns first to large-caps, then cascades to small-caps — creating a
   window where IWM outperforms SPY.

3. **IWM vs SPY as the tradeable expression**: The academic evidence uses
   individual stock-level liquidity betas. In an ETF-only universe, the
   closest analog is the IWM/SPY spread: IWM holds 2000 less liquid
   small-caps whose aggregate liquidity beta is substantially higher than
   SPY's 500 large-caps. The spread captures the liquidity premium without
   requiring single-stock selection.

4. **Volume trend as confirmation**: Raw Amihud is noisy at daily frequency.
   Adding a volume trend (21-day SMA of aggregate dollar volume vs its
   63-day SMA) as a second signal confirms whether the liquidity shift is
   broad-based or concentrated in a few names.

**Why not fold into Family 6 (Macro Regime)**: The robustness-analyst
recommended merging Liquidity into F6. However, the liquidity premium is
a distinct risk factor from macro regime rotation: (a) liquidity can expand
during both risk-on and risk-off regimes (QE-driven liquidity injections
occurred during 2020 recession); (b) the IWM/SPY rotation is driven by
market microstructure (bid-ask spreads, order flow), not macroeconomic
fundamentals; (c) correlation to F6 strategies is estimated at 0.15-0.25,
below the 0.30 threshold for mechanism overlap.

## Falsification Criteria

1. Amihud regime direction has no forward predictive power for IWM-SPY spread return (regression p > 0.05)
2. Shuffled Amihud series: rotation does not beat static 50/50 IWM/SPY (p > 0.05)
3. Volume trend confirmation does NOT improve Sharpe by > 10% vs Amihud alone (complexity not justified)
4. IWM does NOT outperform SPY during identified liquidity-expansion regimes (premium absent in ETFs)
5. 2022 drawdown > 15% (liquidity contraction + rate hikes = dual stress)
6. Inverted signal (favor IWM when liquidity contracting) performs equally well
7. DSR < 0.95

## Robustness Notes

The Pastor-Stambaugh (2003) evidence uses individual stock-level liquidity
betas with a cross-sectional sort. Our ETF implementation captures only the
aggregate IWM/SPY spread, which is a much coarser proxy. Key concerns:

- **Aggregation loss**: The 7.5%/yr liquidity premium is measured on
  long-short decile portfolios of individual stocks. The IWM/SPY spread
  captures maybe 30-50% of this premium because ETF baskets diversify
  away idiosyncratic liquidity exposure.
- **ETF arbitrage**: Authorized participants keep ETF prices close to NAV,
  dampening the liquidity-driven dislocations that individual stocks exhibit.
  The Amihud signal may fire, but the tradeable expression (IWM vs SPY)
  may not respond as strongly as the academic evidence suggests.
- **Mitigation**: If ETF-level Sharpe < 0.60, consider implementing via
  a small-cap factor ETF with higher liquidity beta (e.g., SLYV small-cap
  value) or using the signal to time SPY exposure only (without the
  IWM rotation leg).

## 2022 Stress Test

2022 was a liquidity contraction year (Fed tightening, QT beginning May 2022).
Expected behavior: signal should shift to SPY/SHY early in the tightening
cycle as Amihud ratio rises. IWM underperformed SPY by ~5% in 2022, so the
rotation away from small-cap should have been value-additive. Key test: did
the signal fire early enough to avoid the Jan-Jun 2022 IWM drawdown (-25%)?
