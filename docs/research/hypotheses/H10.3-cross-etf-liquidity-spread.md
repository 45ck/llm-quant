# Hypothesis H10.3: Cross-ETF Liquidity Spread Premium

hypothesis_id: "H10.3"
family: 10 -- Liquidity
created_at: "2026-03-31"
status: "hypothesis_written"

## Statement

Dollar-neutral spreads between more-liquid and less-liquid ETF pairs (IWM/SPY,
EEM/EFA, HYG/LQD), entered when an aggregate liquidity index signals improving
conditions, earn a liquidity premium. When the aggregate Amihud index (cross-asset
average) is declining AND the VIX term structure is in contango (no crisis), go
long the less-liquid leg and short the more-liquid leg. Exit when liquidity
deteriorates or VIX flips to backwardation.

## Mechanism

Less-liquid assets earn a persistent liquidity premium (Amihud 2002, Pastor-Stambaugh
2003). This premium varies through time: it compresses when aggregate liquidity is
abundant (less-liquid assets appreciate relative to more-liquid peers) and expands
during liquidity crises (less-liquid assets sell off harder).

The strategy times the liquidity premium using two signals:

1. **Aggregate Amihud declining**: Cross-asset liquidity is improving. The
   liquidity premium is being harvested -- less-liquid assets are catching up.
   Timing into this signal captures the premium without holding through
   liquidity crises.

2. **VIX contango**: The term structure being in contango (VIX3M > VIX) confirms
   no acute crisis is underway. This is a safety filter -- during crises, liquidity
   premiums invert (less-liquid assets get destroyed).

Three independent spreads diversify single-pair risk:
- **IWM/SPY**: Size-based liquidity spread (small vs large cap)
- **EEM/EFA**: Geography-based liquidity spread (emerging vs developed)
- **HYG/LQD**: Credit quality liquidity spread (high-yield vs investment-grade)

Each spread is dollar-neutral and approximately beta-neutral (hedge ratio via
rolling 63-day OLS).

## Measurement

- signal:
    - "Aggregate Amihud = mean Amihud across SPY, QQQ, IWM, EFA, EEM, HYG, LQD"
    - "Amihud improving = 21d avg < 63d avg (declining trend)"
    - "VIX contango = VIX3M/VIX > 1.0"
- entry: "When BOTH conditions met: go long less-liquid legs (IWM, EEM, HYG), short more-liquid legs (SPY, EFA, LQD). Dollar-neutral per pair."
- exit: "Amihud increasing (21d avg > 63d avg) OR VIX backwardation (VIX > VIX3M)"
- hedge_ratio: "Rolling 63-day OLS beta for each pair. Recompute weekly."
- position_sizing: "Each pair gets 1/3 of allocated capital. Dollar-neutral per pair."

## Falsification Criteria

1. Less-liquid legs do NOT outperform more-liquid legs during improving-liquidity periods (p > 0.10)
2. Single-pair strategy (any one pair alone) shows no positive Sharpe -- the effect is pair-specific, not market-wide
3. VIX contango filter does NOT improve risk-adjusted returns by > 10% (crisis filter unnecessary)
4. Aggregate Amihud direction has no forward predictive power for spread returns (regression p > 0.10)
5. HYG/LQD leg correlation to F1 > 0.40 (not genuinely diversifying)
6. DSR < 0.95
