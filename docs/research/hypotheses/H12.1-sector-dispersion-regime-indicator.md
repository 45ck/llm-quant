# Hypothesis H12.1: Sector Dispersion Regime Indicator

hypothesis_id: "H12.1"
family: 12 -- Dispersion
created_at: "2026-03-31"
status: "hypothesis_written"

## Statement

When cross-sector return dispersion z-score exceeds +1.0 (high differentiation),
overweight the top-3 momentum sectors and underweight the bottom-3 (momentum
regime). When dispersion z-score drops below -0.5 (herding / low differentiation),
hold equal-weight SPY (mean-reversion regime). The dispersion level classifies
which strategy regime is operative, improving risk-adjusted returns.

## Mechanism

Stivers (2010, JFM&P) documented that cross-sectional return dispersion predicts
time variation in the value and momentum premiums. The mechanism:

1. **High dispersion = stock-picking regime**: When sectors diverge, information
   is being differentially priced. Momentum strategies work because trends in
   individual sectors/stocks are driven by fundamental repricing, not correlated
   factor moves.

2. **Low dispersion = beta regime**: When all sectors move together (low
   dispersion), the market is driven by systematic risk factors (macro, rates,
   sentiment). Individual momentum signals are noise in this regime -- equal-weight
   or defensive positioning outperforms.

3. **Dispersion as a leading indicator**: Dispersion tends to rise BEFORE momentum
   starts working and fall BEFORE momentum crashes (correlation spike events).
   This gives a timing edge for rotating between momentum and passive strategies.
