## Statement

Commodity ETFs in backwardation (positive carry) deliver excess returns relative
to those in contango (negative carry), because backwardation compensates holders
for bearing spot price risk while contango reflects storage costs and insurance
premiums paid by hedgers. A strategy that goes long USO when its roll yield proxy
is positive (backwardation) and stays flat when roll yield is negative (contango)
generates Sharpe > 0.80 with MaxDD < 15%.

## Mechanism

Koijen, Moskowitz, Pedersen, and Vrugt (JFE 2018) demonstrated that carry —
defined as the expected return assuming prices stay constant — predicts returns
across equities, bonds, FX, and commodities with portfolio Sharpe ~0.7. For
commodities specifically, carry manifests through the futures term structure:

- **Backwardation** (spot > futures): positive carry. Producers hedge by selling
  futures below spot, creating a risk premium for long holders. The futures price
  converges up toward spot as expiration approaches. This is the Keynes-Hicks
  "normal backwardation" hypothesis.
- **Contango** (futures > spot): negative carry. Storage costs and convenience
  yield dynamics make holding the commodity ETF a losing proposition as the fund
  rolls contracts at a loss (selling cheap near-month, buying expensive far-month).

The ETF-implementable signal: USO's return divergence between 21-day and 63-day
momentum captures the term structure slope. When short-term momentum exceeds
long-term momentum, the commodity is in a demand-driven regime consistent with
backwardation. When short-term lags long-term, contango drag is dominating.

**Why this works at ETF level:** USO tracks WTI crude oil front-month futures.
Its total return includes the roll yield — negative in contango (most of 2015-2020),
positive in backwardation (2021-2022 energy crisis). The 21d vs 63d momentum
divergence captures this asymmetry without requiring direct futures curve data.

**Why this is NOT F3 (momentum):** Pure momentum goes long when price is rising
and short when falling. Carry goes long when the *yield structure* is favorable
regardless of price direction. Oil can be rising in price but in deep contango
(negative carry) — momentum says long, carry says flat. The signals are
near-orthogonal (expected rho to F3: -0.05 to +0.10).

## Falsification Criteria

1. Shuffled returns test: p-value > 0.05 (carry signal has no predictive power for USO returns)
2. Carry_score does not predict forward 5d returns better than random (regression t-stat < 2.0)
3. Strategy returns in backwardation periods are not significantly different from contango periods (the mechanism is not operating)
4. 2020 oil crash: strategy must have been flat or short entering the April 2020 WTI negative price event — if it was long, the signal is not capturing term structure correctly
5. 2022 drawdown > 15% (fails Track A MaxDD gate during commodity supercycle)
6. DSR < 0.95 after multiple-testing correction

## 2022 Survival Assessment

Mixed. 2022 was a commodity supercycle year (Russia-Ukraine, energy crisis). USO
rose ~30% before falling ~40% from June peak. A carry signal should have been
long during the backwardation-driven rally (H1 2022) and exited when contango
returned (H2 2022). If the 21d/63d divergence signal properly tracks the term
structure, the strategy should have captured H1 gains and avoided H2 drawdown.
Risk: the speed of the June-December reversal may have caused whipsaw losses.
