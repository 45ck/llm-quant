**New Blocker — Lookback Sensitivity:** The 120-day lookback is a sharp narrow peak.
Full sensitivity sweep (lookback_days → Sharpe):
- lb=60: 0.325 | lb=90: 0.049 | lb=100: 0.431 | lb=110: 0.269
- lb=120: 0.923 (base) | lb=130: 0.919 | lb=150: 0.309 | lb=180: 0.319

The 120-130d window works; everything else collapses. This is classic lookback optimization bias.
At lb=90, Sharpe=0.049 (near-zero) — the strategy barely works.

**Root cause:** The 2022 simultaneous SPY+TLT bear market creates a narrow rotation timing window. The 120d lookback arrives at the SPY→GLD/BIL rotation exactly right; 90d is too early (re-enters SPY), 150d is too slow (stays in SPY too long).

**CPCV strength:** OOS/IS=1.076 (OOS Sharpe=0.993 > IS Sharpe=0.923) — positive signal but doesn't overcome perturbation failure.

**Perturbation results:**
- lookback_days=90:  Sharpe=0.049 (-94.7%) UNSTABLE
- lookback_days=150: Sharpe=0.309 (-66.5%) UNSTABLE
- rebalance_freq=10: Sharpe=0.620 (-32.8%) UNSTABLE
- top_k=2:           Sharpe=1.030 (+11.6%) STABLE
- rank_by=sharpe:    Sharpe=1.118 (+21.1%) STABLE

**Decision: DO NOT ADVANCE.** Perturbation gate failed (2/5 < 3/5).

**Future approach for this hypothesis:** Test dual-momentum (Antonacci): absolute + relative momentum. Use GLD/SPY/TLT/BIL universe but only enter if 6-month return > 1-month T-bill rate (absolute momentum gate). This has stronger theoretical grounding and may be less lookback-sensitive because the absolute momentum condition provides a natural regime filter.

**UNIQUE FINDING — HY vs IG Signal Horizon Asymmetry:**

| Signal Window | IG Credit (AGG/LQD/VCIT) | HY Credit (HYG) |
|--------------|--------------------------|-----------------|
| sw=3 | ALWAYS UNSTABLE (-75 to -100%) | **STABLE (-4.9%)** |
| sw=7 | Often stable | **CATASTROPHICALLY UNSTABLE (-102.3%, Sharpe=-0.020)** |

This is the **opposite** of every IG credit strategy. The theoretical interpretation:

- **HY credit (HYG):** Reflects near-term default risk appetite. Junk bond default risk horizon = months. Signal is actionable at 3-day resolution. At 7-day resolution, risk sentiment has already been priced into QQQ before the signal triggers.
- **IG credit (AGG/LQD):** Reflects longer-term rate and credit cycle dynamics. Needs 5-7 days to clear noise; at 3 days it's too noisy.

This asymmetry provides **theoretical validation** of the mechanism: different information half-lives, different optimal signal windows. The credit-equity alpha is genuinely rooted in cross-asset information flow.

**Performance note:** Sharpe=0.867 is the lowest in the credit-equity passing group. This may reflect that HY credit adds higher-frequency noise that partially cancels the directional signal over a 5-day window. Lower alpha than IG credit signals for equity prediction.

**Correlation note:** HYG-QQQ shares the identical HYG entry/exit signal as HYG-SPY 5d. When HYG-QQQ enters, HYG-SPY also enters simultaneously. Portfolio construction must account for this — not two independent alpha sources.

**CPCV OOS/IS = 1.050:** Eighth consecutive credit strategy with OOS/IS > 1.0.
