## System Context

This research targets **llm-quant**, a systematic macro trading system managing a $100,000 paper trading portfolio across 39 instruments in six asset classes: US equity ETFs (SPY, QQQ, IWM, DIA, VTI, IVV, XLK, XLF, XLE, XLV, XLI, XLY, XLP, XLU, XLRE, XLB, XLC), international equity (VGK, EEM, EFA), fixed income (TLT, IEF, SHY, LQD, HYG, TIP), commodities (GLD, SLV, USO), crypto (BTC-USD, ETH-USD, SOL-USD, XRP-USD, ADA-USD), and forex (EURUSD=X, GBPUSD=X, USDJPY=X, AUDUSD=X, USDCHF=X).

### Trading Hours by Asset Class

| Asset Class | Trading Hours | Yahoo Finance Daily Bar Close |
|---|---|---|
| US Equity, Intl Equity, Fixed Income, Commodities | 9:30 AM - 4:00 PM ET, Mon-Fri | 4:00 PM ET |
| **Crypto (5 instruments)** | **24/7/365** | ~00:00 UTC (midnight) |
| **Forex (5 instruments)** | **24/5** (Sun 5 PM - Fri 5 PM ET) | ~22:00 UTC (5:00 PM ET) |

### Relevant Code Paths

- **Data ingestion**: `src/llm_quant/data/fetcher.py` calls `yfinance.download(interval="1d")` for all 39 instruments. No intraday data is fetched or stored.
- **Indicator computation**: `src/llm_quant/data/indicators.py` computes SMA(20/50/200), RSI(14), MACD(12,26,9), ATR(14) from daily closes via `compute_indicators()`. No concept of intraday signal updates.
- **Signal generation**: `scripts/build_context.py` runs the pipeline (fetch if stale, compute indicators, build context) and outputs JSON. The LLM generates signals from this daily snapshot. No mechanism exists to update signals between daily runs.
- **Execution**: `scripts/execute_decision.py` fills at the latest available close. For crypto and forex, this means the signal is computed from yesterday's bar close, and execution happens potentially 12-24 hours later.
- **Regime classification**: `src/llm_quant/brain/context.py` uses VIX and TLT/SHY yield spread to classify regimes. Daily data only; no intraday regime updates.

### Risk Parameters (from `config/risk.toml`)

- Crypto: max 5% position weight per instrument, 15% stop-loss (wider for volatility)
- Forex: max 8% position weight per instrument, 3% stop-loss (tighter for lower vol)
- Combined crypto + forex: up to 10 instruments, potentially 25-40% of portfolio if fully allocated

### Current Gap

The system treats all 39 instruments identically: daily bar close generates signals, execution follows at the next opportunity. For US equity ETFs, the market is closed between signal generation and execution, so minimal information arrives. But for crypto and forex:

- **Crypto trades 24/7**: Between midnight UTC bar close and our next execution (typically the following US afternoon), 14-18 hours of continuous trading occurs. Price discovery happens around the clock -- Asian and European sessions contribute information our daily bar misses.
- **Forex trades 24/5**: Between 5 PM ET close and our next execution, the Asian and European sessions provide new macro information. BOJ, ECB, and RBA announcements occur during these sessions.
- **Weekend crypto gap**: 48-72 hours of price action between Friday's close and Monday's signal is invisible to the system.

By the time we act on a crypto or forex momentum signal, the edge may have partially or fully decayed.

## Research Question

For crypto and forex instruments, **how much alpha decay occurs between EOD signal generation and next execution** -- and is there a low-overhead way to capture intraday signal updates without re-architecting the system?

Specifically:

1. What is the information coefficient (IC) of a daily momentum signal measured at bar close versus N hours later (N = 1, 2, 4, 8, 12, 24)?
2. For crypto, how much of the close-to-close return is realized in the first 4 hours after UTC midnight versus the remaining 20 hours?
3. For forex, does the Asian session systematically move prices in the direction of the prior day's momentum signal, eroding the edge before New York opens?
4. Is there a single "optimal signal time" per instrument that minimizes decay?
5. What is the weekend gap effect for crypto, and does it systematically oppose Friday's signal direction?

---

## Hypothesis

Momentum signals on BTC, ETH, and major forex pairs decay meaningfully within 4-8 hours of generation. Decay is asymmetric: strong signals (highest conviction) decay faster because they attract faster-moving participants. Weak signals (near noise) may not decay at all.

**Magnitude predictions**:

- **Crypto**: 15-25% reduction in forward 24-hour signal return between midnight UTC close and 12:00 noon UTC. BTC/ETH decay faster than SOL/XRP/ADA due to deeper institutional coverage and cross-exchange arbitrage.
- **Forex**: 10-20% reduction between 5 PM ET close and next-day NY open (9:30 AM ET). Asian and European sessions partially incorporate the information captured by our daily momentum signal.
- **Weekend crypto**: 25-40% reduction in Monday signal effectiveness versus Tuesday-Friday, because Saturday-Sunday price action (invisible to the system) either continues or reverses the trend.

**Proposed fix**: Keep crypto signal at 00:00 UTC but add a secondary signal check at 12:00 UTC using 12-hour bars. For forex, execute at London open (13:00 UTC / 8:00 AM ET) rather than waiting for NY afternoon. Add a "signal freshness" flag to the execution pipeline -- stale signals get reduced conviction or rejection.

**Implementation complexity**: Low. Requires fetching hourly OHLCV for 10 instruments, computing simplified indicators on hourly bars, and adding a freshness check. No fundamental architecture change.

## Methodology

### Phase 1: Hourly Data Collection (Week 1)

Symbol mapping: BTC-USD -> BTCUSDT, ETH-USD -> ETHUSDT, SOL-USD -> SOLUSDT, XRP-USD -> XRPUSDT, ADA-USD -> ADAUSDT. Fetch 2 years (~17,520 candles/symbol, 18 paginated requests each).

**Forex hourly data**: Yahoo Finance via `yfinance.download(interval="1h")` (730 days max). Alternative: OANDA v20 API with free practice account (10+ years history). Symbols: EURUSD=X, GBPUSD=X, USDJPY=X, AUDUSD=X, USDCHF=X. All timestamps in UTC. Remove Saturday/Sunday bars (market closed).

**Storage**: New DuckDB table `market_data_hourly(symbol, timestamp, open, high, low, close, volume)`. Validate by aggregating hourly closes to daily and cross-referencing against existing `market_data_daily`.

### Phase 2: Signal Decay Measurement (Weeks 2-3)

#### 2a. IC Decay Curves

For each instrument, compute daily momentum signal at bar close:

```
signal_t = sign(close_t - sma_20_t)
```

Measure forward returns at various horizons using hourly prices:

```
fwd_return_Nh = (price_{t + N hours} / close_t) - 1
```

Compute Spearman rank IC between signal and forward return for N = 1, 2, 4, 8, 12, 24, 48 hours. IC significance threshold: `IC > 2 / sqrt(n - 3)` (for n=500: IC > 0.090; for n=250: IC > 0.127).

Repeat with alternative signals:
- `signal_macd = sign(macd_t)` -- MACD direction
- `signal_rsi = -1 if rsi > 70 else +1 if rsi < 30 else 0` -- mean-reversion RSI
- `signal_composite = signal_sma + signal_macd + signal_rsi` -- combined

Plot IC decay curves per instrument, annotated with the hour at which IC drops below 70% of the t=0 value.

#### 2b. Intraday Return Decomposition

For each crypto instrument, decompose daily close-to-close return into 24 hourly buckets:

```
hourly_contrib_h = (close_hour_h - close_hour_{h-1}) / close_t    for h in [1, 24]
```

Aggregate across all days to produce an average hourly return profile. Key questions: Is crypto price discovery concentrated in specific sessions (Asian, European, US)? Does the first 4 hours after UTC midnight carry more signal-aligned return than the remaining 20 hours?

For forex, decompose into three sessions:
- **Asian**: 22:00 UTC to 07:00 UTC (Tokyo)
- **European**: 07:00 UTC to 12:00 UTC (London)
- **US**: 12:00 UTC to 22:00 UTC (New York)

Compute `signal_aligned_pct` = fraction of sessions where the return direction matches the daily signal direction.

#### 2c. Weekend Gap Analysis (Crypto Only)

For each crypto instrument:

1. Friday close to Monday close return.
2. Classify weekends as continuation (price moved with Friday signal) or reversal (moved against).
3. Compare Monday morning IC vs. Tuesday-Friday IC. If Friday signals lose predictive power over the weekend, a policy change is needed.
4. Compute weekend return magnitude: median, mean, and 95th percentile in absolute basis points.

### Phase 3: Optimal Signal Time (Week 3)

For each instrument, compute the momentum signal at every hour of the day using hourly rolling indicators, and measure IC of each signal hour against the subsequent 24-hour return:

```
For each hour h in [0, 23]:
    signal_h = sign(close_hour_h - sma_20_at_hour_h)
    fwd_return = close_{hour_h + 24} / close_hour_h - 1
    IC_h = spearman_corr(signal_h, fwd_return) across all days
```

Evaluate candidate signal times:

| Candidate | Time (UTC) | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| Current (crypto) | 00:00 | Yahoo Finance daily bar close |
| Current (forex) | 22:00 (5 PM ET) | Yahoo Finance daily bar close |
| Asian open | 01:00 | Captures post-midnight price discovery |
| London open | 08:00 | Major liquidity injection, European macro releases |
| NY open | 13:30 | Deepest combined liquidity for crypto and forex |
| London fix | 16:00 | WM/Reuters fix, forex reference rate |
| Dual signal | 00:00 + 12:00 | Two signals/day, execute the fresher one |

For the "dual signal" approach, quantify: IC improvement vs. single signal, whipsaw frequency (how often the two signals disagree), and implementation effort.

### Phase 4: Recommendation and Integration Design (Week 4)

1. Compile per-instrument recommendation table with optimal signal time, execution window, and weekend policy.
2. Draft minimal code changes: estimate total lines of code and identify specific functions to modify.
3. Define weekend crypto policy: (a) generate a Saturday signal from weekend data, (b) reduce Monday position sizes by a fixed percentage, (c) skip Monday crypto signals entirely, or (d) no change if weekend decay is immaterial.

## Literature Review

**Moskowitz, Ooi & Pedersen (2012)** -- "Time Series Momentum." *Journal of Financial Economics*, 104(2), 228-250. Foundational paper on time-series momentum across equities, bonds, commodities, and currencies. Documents that momentum signals have a characteristic decay profile -- strongest at measurement time, decaying toward zero over the holding period. Figure 3 shows ~60% of monthly alpha captured in the first month. Extract: the alpha decay curve shape and cross-asset comparison. Limitation: operates at monthly frequency; daily-to-hourly dynamics may differ materially.

**Biais, Bisiere, Bouvard & Casamatta (2023)** -- "Equilibrium Bitcoin Pricing." *Journal of Finance*, 78(2), 967-1014. Theoretical model of BTC price formation accounting for 24/7 trading, miner behavior, and absence of fundamental anchoring (unlike equities). Extract: predictions about whether information incorporation is continuous (gradual signal decay) or bursty (session-concentrated decay). If continuous, shifting signal time has less impact; if bursty, the right signal time matters greatly.

**Ranaldo & Soderlind (2010)** -- "Safe Haven Currencies." *Review of Finance*, 14(3), 385-407. Safe-haven currencies (CHF, JPY) appreciate during stress; risk-sensitive currencies (AUD, GBP) depreciate. Extract: session-level return decomposition. If Asian sessions systematically amplify JPY signals (when yen liquidity is deepest), delayed execution captures more of the move. If European sessions reverse signals, early execution is better. Directly informs the optimal execution time for USDJPY=X and USDCHF=X.

**Additional references**:
- Andersen, Bollerslev, Diebold & Vega (2003), "Micro Effects of Macro Announcements," *American Economic Review*, 93(1) -- FX prices fully adjust within 5-15 minutes of macro releases; daily signals cannot capture news-driven alpha.
- Liu & Tsyvinski (2021), "Risks and Returns of Cryptocurrency," *Review of Financial Studies*, 34(6) -- crypto momentum strongest at 1-4 week horizons, weaker at daily frequency. Important calibration for expected IC magnitudes.
- Makarov & Schoar (2020), "Trading and Arbitrage in Cryptocurrency Markets," *Journal of Financial Economics*, 135(2) -- persistent cross-exchange price deviations (up to 5% in 2017, <50 bp by 2019). If arbitrage is not instantaneous, our signal may capture information not yet arbitraged across all venues.

## Evaluation Criteria

### Success Thresholds

| Metric | Threshold | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| IC decay at 8 hours | IC drops below 70% of t=0 for >= 3 of 10 instruments | Confirms meaningful decay exists |
| Optimal time improvement | IC improves >= 10% for >= 3 of 10 instruments at optimal time | Below 10%, noise dominates the improvement |
| Weekend gap magnitude | Weekend return exceeds 100 bp absolute >= 30% of weekends | Material gap warrants a policy change |
| Backtest Sharpe improvement | Strategy B Sharpe exceeds Strategy A by >= 0.1 for crypto/forex sub-portfolio | Below 0.1, implementation is not justified |
| Implementation effort | Total code changes < 500 lines across all files | "Low-overhead" claim must hold |

### Failure Conditions

- **If IC does not decay meaningfully** (IC at 8h remains above 85% of t=0 for all instruments): Daily signals are sufficient for 24-hour markets. No change needed.
- **If optimal signal time equals current time** (00:00 UTC for crypto, 22:00 UTC for forex): System is already configured correctly.
- **If hourly data is too noisy** (IC standard errors overlap zero at all horizons): Decay dynamics are unobservable with our data. Fall back to daily-only analysis.
- **If implementation exceeds 500 lines**: Re-scope as a separate project rather than an incremental improvement.

### Dollar Reality Check

Max crypto+forex allocation: ~$65k (5 crypto at 5% + 5 forex at 8%). If alpha recovery is 10 bp/trade at 1x/month turnover, annual benefit is ~$78. The research may validly conclude that the improvement does not justify implementation cost. That is a useful finding, not a failure.

## Anti-Overfitting Warnings

1. **Optimal time is path-dependent**: Changing signal time changes the signal itself (SMA(20) at 12:00 UTC uses different 20-day windows than at 00:00 UTC). Report IC for same parameters at different times; note when parameter changes are also needed.
2. **Hourly data quality**: Binance pre-2020 had quality issues (smaller exchange, different fee structure). Use 2022-present as primary analysis window, pre-2022 as robustness check.
3. **Selection bias in hour optimization**: Testing 24 hours and picking the best is data mining. Apply Bonferroni correction (p < 0.05/24 = 0.002) or use train/validate split (first year identifies, second year validates optimal hour).
4. **Weekend non-stationarity**: Crypto weekend dynamics differ in bull vs. bear markets. Report separately by regime (90-day trailing return > 0 vs. < 0).
5. **Signal type asymmetry**: Momentum signals decay as the trend continues ("used up"). Mean-reversion signals may improve with delay (oversold condition persists). Analyze each type separately.
6. **Liquidity confounding**: Crypto volume peaks during US afternoon. If IC appears higher at high-volume hours, it might reflect better execution (tighter spreads) rather than fresher signals. Separate signal quality from execution quality (Brief 1.1 handles execution).
7. **Survivorship bias in crypto**: ADA and SOL had low liquidity before 2021. Start analysis for these instruments from 2021 onward.
8. **Time zone confusion**: Standardize all analysis to UTC. Document mappings between UTC and exchange session times explicitly.
9. **Dual signal whipsaw**: If recommending two signals per day, quantify disagreement frequency. Whipsaw increases transaction costs and may reduce Sharpe rather than improve it.
