## System Context

This research targets **llm-quant**, a systematic macro paper-trading system managing a $100,000 portfolio across 39 assets spanning six asset classes:

- **US equity ETFs** (17): SPY, QQQ, IWM, DIA, VTI, IVV, XLK, XLF, XLE, XLV, XLI, XLY, XLP, XLU, XLRE, XLB, XLC
- **International equity** (3): VGK, EEM, EFA
- **Fixed income** (6): TLT, IEF, SHY, LQD, HYG, TIP
- **Commodities** (3): GLD, SLV, USO
- **Crypto** (5): BTC-USD, ETH-USD, SOL-USD, XRP-USD, ADA-USD
- **Forex** (5): EURUSD=X, GBPUSD=X, USDJPY=X, AUDUSD=X, USDCHF=X

**Technology stack**: Python 3.12, Polars DataFrames, DuckDB persistent storage, Pydantic configs, Jinja2 prompt templates.

**Current momentum implementation**: The system computes SMA(20) and SMA(50) crossovers plus MACD(12,26,9) in `src/llm_quant/data/indicators.py` (function `compute_indicators()`). These are purely technical indicators -- there is no systematic time-series momentum (TSMOM) signal, no cross-asset momentum ranking, no momentum crash detection, and no crowding measurement. The LLM receives RSI and MACD per-asset in the `MarketContext` dataclass (`src/llm_quant/brain/models.py`) and makes qualitative judgments about momentum without a quantitative TSMOM score.

**Current regime detection**: A simple 2-factor heuristic (VIX level + TLT/SHY yield spread proxy) produces three labels: `risk_on`, `risk_off`, `transition` (file: `src/llm_quant/brain/context.py`). No regime-conditional momentum strategy exists. Brief 5.1 proposes upgrading this to a 4-factor HMM.

**Pipeline**: `scripts/build_context.py` fetches Yahoo Finance data, computes per-asset indicators, builds `MarketContext`, and outputs JSON. The LLM reads this context and produces `TradingDecision` with up to 5 trade signals. Signals pass through 8 pre-trade risk checks (`src/llm_quant/risk/manager.py`) including position size (max 2% NAV per trade), position weight (max 10%), gross/net exposure, sector concentration (max 30%), cash reserve (min 5%), stop-loss requirement, and drawdown circuit breaker (max 15%).

**Data availability**: 252 trading days of daily OHLCV for all 39 assets plus VIX, stored in DuckDB table `market_data_daily`. Indicators (SMA, RSI, MACD, ATR, Bollinger Bands) are computed via Polars and stored alongside price data.

**Performance targets**: Sharpe > 0.8, max drawdown < 15%, Sortino > 1.0, Calmar > 0.5, annualized return 8-15%.

## Research Question

Over what lookback periods does time-series momentum (TSMOM) generate statistically significant risk-adjusted returns across the 39-asset universe, and can a crowding indicator built from freely available proxies (return dispersion, volume anomalies, pairwise correlation) predict momentum crash magnitude and timing with sufficient lead time (2-4 weeks) to reduce the portfolio's exposure before losses materialize?

---

## Hypothesis

**H1 (TSMOM alpha)**: Based on Moskowitz, Ooi & Pedersen (2012), TSMOM signals constructed as the sign of the past 12-month return, applied with a 1-month skip (to avoid microstructure noise), will generate positive Sharpe ratios across all six asset classes in the llm-quant universe. Specifically:
- US equities TSMOM Sharpe: 0.4-0.6 (lower due to high correlation within sector ETFs)
- Fixed income TSMOM Sharpe: 0.3-0.5 (duration momentum is well-documented)
- Commodities TSMOM Sharpe: 0.5-0.8 (strongest historical TSMOM asset class)
- Crypto TSMOM Sharpe: 0.6-1.0 (high volatility amplifies momentum profits but also crash risk)
- Forex TSMOM Sharpe: 0.3-0.5 (carry-adjusted momentum)
- Combined cross-asset TSMOM Sharpe: 0.6-0.9 (diversification benefit)

**H2 (Crash prediction)**: Momentum crashes cluster at regime transitions -- specifically when the market reverses from a strong trend (Daniel & Moskowitz, 2016). A composite crowding index constructed from three freely available proxies will signal elevated crash probability 2-4 weeks before momentum drawdowns exceed 5%:
- **Cross-sectional return dispersion collapse**: When all assets move together (low dispersion), momentum strategies are crowded. A z-score below -1.5 on 60-day rolling dispersion signals crowding.
- **Abnormal volume on momentum leaders**: Z-score of rolling 20-day average volume on the top-quintile momentum assets. A z-score above 2.0 suggests herding behavior.
- **Pairwise equity correlation spike**: Rolling 20-day average pairwise correlation among SPY, QQQ, IWM, EEM, VGK exceeding 0.85 signals a risk-off cascade in which momentum reverses violently.

**H3 (Regime interaction)**: TSMOM performance varies dramatically by regime. In `risk_on` regimes (if Brief 5.1 is implemented), TSMOM alpha is strongest. In regime transitions, TSMOM crashes are most likely. Conditioning momentum signals on the macro regime (from Brief 5.1 or the current 2-factor model) will improve Sharpe by >= 0.1 versus unconditional TSMOM.

**Null hypothesis**: The TSMOM premium has decayed since publication (2012) due to crowding by systematic funds. The freely available crowding proxies have no predictive power for crash timing. The current SMA crossover + MACD signals are sufficient.

## Methodology

### Phase 1: TSMOM Signal Construction (Week 1)

1. **Fetch extended historical data**: Extend the current 252-day lookback to at least 504 trading days (2 years) to compute 12-month momentum with a 1-month skip. Modify `config/default.toml` to set `lookback_days = 504` for this research (or fetch separately into a research-specific DuckDB).

2. **Compute TSMOM signals for multiple lookbacks**: For each of the 39 tradeable assets, compute:
   - `tsmom_1m`: Sign of 1-month (21 trading days) return, skipping most recent 5 days
   - `tsmom_3m`: Sign of 3-month (63 days) return, skipping 5 days
   - `tsmom_6m`: Sign of 6-month (126 days) return, skipping 5 days
   - `tsmom_12m`: Sign of 12-month (252 days) return, skipping 21 days (the canonical TSMOM signal)
   - Each signal is +1 (long) or -1 (short), scaled by inverse volatility (20-day rolling standard deviation of returns) so that each asset contributes equal risk

3. **Cross-sectional momentum (XSMOM)**: Rank all 39 assets by trailing 12-month return. Go long the top quintile (8 assets), short the bottom quintile (8 assets). This complements TSMOM by capturing relative strength.

4. **Composite momentum score**: For each asset, create a blended score:
   ```
   mom_score = 0.4 * tsmom_12m + 0.3 * tsmom_6m + 0.2 * tsmom_3m + 0.1 * tsmom_1m
   ```
   Normalize to [-1, +1] range. This multi-horizon signal reduces sensitivity to any single lookback.

### Phase 2: Crowding Indicator Construction (Week 2)

1. **Cross-sectional return dispersion** (`crowding_dispersion`):
   - For each day, compute the cross-sectional standard deviation of daily returns across all 39 assets
   - Compute the 60-day rolling mean and std of this dispersion series
   - Z-score = (current dispersion - 60d mean) / 60d std
   - Low dispersion (z < -1.5) = crowded; high dispersion (z > 1.5) = dispersed

2. **Volume anomaly on momentum leaders** (`crowding_volume`):
   - Identify the top-5 assets by `mom_score` on each day
   - For each, compute the 20-day rolling average daily dollar volume
   - Z-score each asset's current volume vs. its own 60-day history
   - Average the z-scores across the top-5 leaders
   - High volume z-score (> 2.0) on momentum leaders = potential herding

3. **Pairwise equity correlation** (`crowding_correlation`):
   - Compute rolling 20-day pairwise Pearson correlation among 5 broad equity ETFs: SPY, QQQ, IWM, EEM, VGK
   - Average all 10 pairwise correlations into a single number
   - Values > 0.85 = extreme correlation (all equities moving as one)

4. **Composite crowding index**:
   ```
   crowding_index = -0.4 * crowding_dispersion + 0.3 * crowding_volume + 0.3 * crowding_correlation
   ```
   Higher values = more crowded. Transform to a probability using a logistic function calibrated on training data.

5. **Crash probability**: Define a "momentum crash" as a week where the TSMOM portfolio loses > 3% (roughly 2 standard deviations). Train a logistic regression on the training set:
   ```
   P(crash next 2 weeks) = logistic(crowding_index, VIX_change_5d, yield_curve_slope)
   ```
   Use purged cross-validation (5-fold with 21-day purge gap) to avoid look-ahead bias.

### Phase 3: Backtesting (Week 3)

1. **Walk-forward backtest, 2010-2025**:
   - Training window: expanding, starting with 2005-2009 (5 years minimum)
   - Test window: 1 year rolling forward
   - Recalibrate momentum weights and crowding model parameters annually
   - Transaction costs: 10 bps per leg for ETFs, 20 bps for crypto, 5 bps for forex

2. **Strategy variants to test**:
   - **TSMOM-only**: Pure 12M TSMOM, equal vol-weighted across all 39 assets
   - **Multi-horizon TSMOM**: Composite momentum score with 4 lookbacks
   - **TSMOM + crash filter**: Reduce exposure by 50% when crowding_index > 80th percentile; go to 100% cash when > 95th percentile
   - **TSMOM + regime filter**: Only take TSMOM signals in `risk_on` regime (requires 5.1 or current 2-factor model)
   - **TSMOM + crash + regime**: Full model combining all components

3. **Benchmark comparisons**:
   - 60/40 SPY/TLT (passive multi-asset baseline)
   - Buy-and-hold equal-weight of all 39 assets
   - SMA crossover (current system's implicit momentum strategy)

4. **Metrics**: Sharpe, Sortino, Calmar, max drawdown, annualized return, annualized vol, hit rate, profit factor, average win/loss ratio, maximum consecutive losing weeks, turnover (annual), crash frequency and severity (conditional on crowding signal state).

### Phase 4: Integration and Delivery (Week 4)

1. **Design context enrichment**: Add per-asset `momentum_score` (float, -1 to +1) and portfolio-level `crash_probability` (float, 0 to 1) to the `MarketContext` JSON output.

2. **Design risk integration**: When `crash_probability > 0.7`, the risk manager should automatically:
   - Reduce `max_trade_size` from 2% to 1% of NAV
   - Increase `min_cash_reserve` from 5% to 15%
   - Flag all new momentum-based BUY signals as requiring HIGH conviction

3. **Write configuration and document the upgrade path**.

## Literature Review

### Primary References

1. **Moskowitz, Ooi & Pedersen (2012)**, "Time Series Momentum," *Journal of Financial Economics*, 104(2), 228-250.
   - **Extract**: The canonical TSMOM paper. Documents significant positive returns from going long assets with positive past 12-month returns and short assets with negative past returns, across 58 liquid futures spanning equities, bonds, currencies, and commodities. Key findings: (a) TSMOM is distinct from cross-sectional momentum, (b) profits are not explained by standard risk factors, (c) the 12-month lookback with 1-month skip is the strongest specification. Replicate their signal construction methodology exactly. Note their asset universe is futures-based -- our ETF implementation may have different characteristics due to management fees and tracking error.

2. **Daniel & Moskowitz (2016)**, "Momentum Crashes," *Journal of Financial Economics*, 122(2), 221-247.
   - **Extract**: Documents that momentum strategies experience occasional severe crashes (e.g., 2009 reversal lost ~50% in 3 months). Crashes are predictable: they occur after market downturns followed by sharp reversals, when past losers (which momentum is short) rally violently. Key finding: conditioning on the market state (bear market recoveries) eliminates most crash risk while preserving alpha. The "dynamic momentum" strategy that reduces exposure when crash risk is high generates Sharpe > 1.0. Implement their crash indicator as a benchmark for our crowding-based approach.

3. **Jegadeesh & Titman (1993)**, "Returns to Buying Winners and Selling Losers: Implications for Stock Market Efficiency," *Journal of Finance*, 48(1), 65-91.
   - **Extract**: The foundational cross-sectional momentum paper. While focused on individual stocks (not ETFs or multi-asset), the methodology for forming winner/loser portfolios and measuring holding-period returns is directly applicable. Key parameters: 3-12 month formation period, 3-12 month holding period, 1-week skip. Their finding that momentum profits reverse at 12-18 months motivates our inclusion of shorter lookbacks alongside the 12-month canonical signal.

4. **Asness, Moskowitz & Pedersen (2013)**, "Value and Momentum Everywhere," *Journal of Finance*, 68(3), 929-985.
   - **Extract**: Extends momentum (and value) to every major asset class: US stocks, UK stocks, continental European stocks, Japanese stocks, country equity indices, currencies, government bonds, and commodity futures. Key finding: momentum strategies in different asset classes are positively correlated, suggesting a common factor. This supports our cross-asset TSMOM approach but warns that diversification benefit is lower than naive asset-class count suggests. Their methodology for constructing country/asset-class level momentum factors is directly applicable.

5. **Lou & Polk (2022)**, "Comomentum: Inferring Arbitrage Activity from Return Correlations," *Review of Financial Studies*, 35(7), 3272-3302.
   - **Extract**: Introduces "comomentum" -- the return correlation among momentum-sorted portfolios -- as a measure of crowding by arbitrageurs. Key finding: when comomentum is high (momentum-winner and momentum-loser portfolios are highly correlated with each other), subsequent momentum returns are lower. This provides the theoretical foundation for our crowding indicator and suggests that return correlation among momentum leaders/laggards is a better crowding proxy than volume alone. Implement their comomentum measure as a cross-validation for our composite crowding index.

### Supplementary References

6. **Baltas & Kosowski (2020)**, "Demystifying Time-Series Momentum Strategies: Volatility Estimators, Trading Rules and Pairwise Correlations," *Journal of Financial Markets*, 50, 100561.
   - **Extract**: Comprehensive sensitivity analysis of TSMOM to implementation choices (volatility estimator, rebalancing frequency, transaction costs). Key finding: TSMOM is robust to most implementation choices but sensitive to the volatility scaling methodology. Use their recommendation of EWMA(60-day) volatility scaling rather than simple rolling standard deviation.

7. **Barroso & Santa-Clara (2015)**, "Momentum Has Its Moments," *Journal of Financial Economics*, 116(1), 111-120.
   - **Extract**: Risk-managed momentum that scales exposure inversely with realized momentum volatility. This approach complements our crowding indicator by providing a non-crash-specific risk adjustment. Sharpe improves from 0.5 to 0.9 in their US equity sample.

8. **Huang, Song & Xiang (2024)**, "Fragile Factor Premia," *Journal of Financial Economics*, 154, 103810.
   - **Extract**: Documents that factor premia (including momentum) weaken substantially after controlling for transaction costs and implementation constraints in the post-2010 era. Essential for calibrating realistic performance expectations in our backtest.

- TSMOM 12M Sharpe (gross) < 0.3 across the full sample -- momentum premium has decayed below viability

- Transaction costs consume > 50% of gross TSMOM returns (the strategy is not implementable in an ETF portfolio)
- The regime interaction (H3) adds no value over unconditional TSMOM, suggesting regime conditioning is unnecessary for momentum strategies

## Anti-Overfitting Warnings

### Specific Pitfalls for This Research

1. **Lookback selection bias**: Testing 4 lookback periods (1M, 3M, 6M, 12M) and optimizing their blend weights on historical data is a classic overfitting vector. **Mitigation**: Fix the blend weights at the theoretically motivated values (heavier on longer lookbacks per Moskowitz et al.) before running the backtest. Report individual lookback results separately so the reader can assess whether blending genuinely helps vs. data-mining.

2. **Crowding threshold calibration**: Setting the "high crowding" threshold at the 80th percentile is arbitrary. If this threshold is optimized on the full sample, the backtest is contaminated. **Mitigation**: Calibrate thresholds on the first 5-year training window (2005-2009) only. Use walk-forward expansion to test whether the threshold remains stable as the training window grows.

3. **Transaction cost sensitivity**: TSMOM strategies with monthly rebalancing generate 200-600% annual turnover. Small changes in assumed transaction costs (5 bps vs. 20 bps) can flip the strategy from profitable to unprofitable. **Mitigation**: Report net Sharpe at three cost levels (5 bps, 10 bps, 20 bps). Identify the break-even transaction cost. If break-even is below 10 bps, the strategy is fragile.

4. **Survivorship bias in ETF universe**: Our 39-asset universe contains only currently listed ETFs and crypto assets. Some assets (especially crypto) did not exist for the full backtest period (e.g., SOL launched in 2020, ADA in 2017). **Mitigation**: For each asset, begin inclusion in the backtest only from its actual listing date. Report backtest results for sub-universes (equity-only, equity+bonds, full universe) to isolate the impact of adding recently-listed assets.

5. **Short-side implementation**: TSMOM is a long-short strategy by construction, but the llm-quant system currently implements long-only trades (the `Action` enum includes SELL/CLOSE but these reduce existing positions, they don't establish new shorts). **Mitigation**: Test both long-only TSMOM (go long winners, hold cash for losers) and long-short TSMOM. Report both results clearly. The integration deliverable should use long-only signals unless the system is extended to support short-selling.

6. **Multiple testing problem**: Testing 5 strategy variants, 4 lookback periods, 3 crowding components, and 6 asset classes generates dozens of statistical tests. By chance, some will appear significant. **Mitigation**: Apply Bonferroni correction (or the less conservative Holm-Bonferroni) to all reported p-values. Focus on the pre-registered primary hypothesis (12M TSMOM + composite crowding) rather than cherry-picking the best variant.

7. **Momentum decay post-publication**: Momentum returns have declined in the post-2012 period as more capital has chased the factor. The 2010-2012 early portion of our backtest predates the paper's wide dissemination. **Mitigation**: Report performance separately for 2010-2015 vs. 2016-2020 vs. 2021-2025 to assess whether the premium is decaying. If post-2020 performance is materially weaker, document this as a limitation and adjust expected forward performance accordingly.

8. **Correlation regime dependence**: In crisis periods, correlations among assets spike toward 1.0, which simultaneously (a) makes the crowding correlation indicator fire and (b) makes all momentum signals reverse together. The crowding indicator may be measuring the same thing as the crash itself, rather than predicting it. **Mitigation**: Test whether the crowding signal has predictive power when conditioned on VIX level being below 25 (i.e., does it predict crashes that start from calm markets, not just confirm ongoing stress?). This is the true test of its value.
